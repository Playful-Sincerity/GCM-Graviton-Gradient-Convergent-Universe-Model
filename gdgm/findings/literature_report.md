# PART 1 — PARTICLE PHYSICS FINDINGS

## 1a. Preon / Sub-lepton Theories

### What Exists

**Pati-Salam Model (1974):** SU(4) × SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R gauge group, treats leptons as a fourth "color" of quark. Technically a GUT rather than a preon model proper, but introduced sub-lepton compositeness into formal physics. Predicts proton decay in ranges still being tested.

**Rishon Model (Harari 1979, Shupe 1979 — independently):** Two preons: T (charge +1/3) and V (charge 0), in ordered triplets. Electron = VVV, electron neutrino = TTT̄, up quarks = TTV permutations. Extended by Harari-Seiberg with hypercolor gauge interaction. Most minimal sub-lepton model in the literature. Closest prior work to GDGM's single-particle proposal.

### Experimental Constraints

- **LHC compositeness scale:** Lower limits >37 TeV (eeqq channels), >40 TeV (μμqq). Any preon binding must happen at energies ≥37,000× proton rest mass.
- **Electron radius:** Constrained to r_e < ~10⁻²² m from g-2 precision measurements. Electron remains pointlike to at least 10⁻²⁴ m.
- **HERA:** No sub-structure sign up to Q² < 5 × 10⁴ GeV².

### Why Preon Models Failed

1. **Binding Energy Hierarchy (most severe):** Electron mass (~0.5 MeV) composed of preons at >37 TeV requires binding energy to cancel preon masses to 10⁸ decimal places. The fine-tuning problem is pushed deeper, not solved.
2. **Spin-statistics / Generation Problem:** μ → eγ decay predicted at rates far above observed limit (<4.2 × 10⁻¹³). Never seen. Devastating.
3. **Proton Decay:** Shared preon constituents of quarks and leptons should produce baryon-violating decays. Constrained to >10³⁴ years.
4. **No Predictive Novelty:** Reproduces SM quantum numbers but adds no verified new predictions.

### What Remains Open

- Compositeness above ~37 TeV formally consistent with all data. Future colliders (100 TeV FCC) could probe.
- Generation problem (why 3 families?) is completely unresolved in SM. Genuine motivation for sub-structure.
- GDGM's density gradient approach is structurally different from discrete preon models — doesn't face same spin-statistics objections. Must address equivalent questions about fermion quantum numbers emerging from bosonic density field.

**Key Citations:** Harari (1979) PLB 86:83; Shupe (1979) PLB 86:87; Pati & Salam (1974) PRD 10:275; PDG (2024).

Derived full Einstein field equations from:
1. Entropy proportional to horizon area (S ∝ A)
2. Thermodynamic relation δQ = T dS at local Rindler horizons

GR is the ONLY consistent equation of state if entropy-area relation holds locally for all causal horizons. Gravity is a thermodynamic phenomenon, not a fundamental force. Thousands of citations, never seriously challenged.

**For GDGM:** Proof-of-concept that GR can emerge from something deeper. However, Jacobson's derivation requires quantum mechanics (Unruh temperature). A purely classical density gradient approach must derive the same result without invoking quantum Unruh radiation.

### Verlinde's Entropic Gravity (2010, 2016)

Gravity as entropic force — statistical tendency toward entropy increase at holographic screens. 2016 extension explains galactic rotation curves without dark matter.

**Tests:** Leiden study (Brouwer 2017) found "good agreement" with Verlinde's lensing predictions for 33,000+ galaxies. Princeton study (2017) showed inconsistency with dwarf galaxy rotation velocities. Cluster environments: only a few can be well reproduced.

**Current status:** Theoretically interesting, empirically mixed. Not a complete or accepted theory.

### String Theory

Single fundamental object (string); different vibration modes = different particles. Gauge symmetries emerge from consistency conditions + compactified extra dimensions. Force differentiation via geometry of extra dimensions, not 3+1D density gradients. Mathematically rigorous but no unique experimental predictions in 40 years.

## 1c. Force Unification and the Symmetry Problem

### Standard Model Symmetry Structure

SM gauge group: SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1). Not derived from first principles — empirically fitted. Each factor has different mathematical structure.

- **U(1):** Simple phase rotation. Electromagnetic + hypercharge.
- **SU(2)_L:** Acts ONLY on left-handed fermions. Maximal parity violation — built in by hand. Why? Unknown.
- **SU(3):** Three-dimensional complex unitary rotations on quarks.
- **Gravity:** Diffeomorphism invariance — not a Yang-Mills gauge symmetry. Structurally different from all three SM forces.

"Why this particular product group?" — genuinely unanswered. One of the deepest open questions in physics.

### Grand Unified Theories

- **SU(5) (Georgi-Glashow 1974):** Simplest GUT. Predicts proton lifetime ~10³¹ years. Super-Kamiokande found >10³⁴ years. **Ruled out.**
- **SO(10):** More promising. One generation per 16D representation (includes right-handed neutrino). Proton decay predictions 10³⁴–10³⁶ years — being tested by HyperKamiokande and DUNE.
- **SUSY GUTs:** Allow better coupling unification. Proton lifetime 10³⁶–10³⁹ years. Under pressure as LHC found no SUSY particles.

Three SM couplings converge at ~10¹⁶ GeV if SUSY assumed — suggestive but not conclusive.

### Parity Violation — The Left-Handedness Problem

Weak force acts exclusively on left-handed particles. Confirmed since 1957 (Wu experiment). No accepted mechanism generates this from a symmetric starting point. Proposals: spontaneous parity breaking (L-R symmetric models), CP violation (CKM matrix). The observed matter-antimatter asymmetry is not explained by known CP violation — off by 10⁸.

**Critical obstacle for GDGM:** A single symmetric gravitational substrate must produce maximal left-handedness as emergent phenomenon. No mechanism for this exists in the literature.

### Quantum Gravity Status (2025)

- **Loop Quantum Gravity:** Quantizes geometry directly. Has not reproduced GR in classical limit with full generality. No unique falsifiable prediction.
- **String Theory:** No unique tested prediction in 40+ years. Strings 2025 found no venue for Strings 2026.
- **Causal dynamical triangulations, spin foams, asymptotic safety:** Technical progress, no experimental contact.

Quantum gravity landscape genuinely open. No approach accepted or empirically successful.

## 1d. Repulsion as Competing Attraction

**No major peer-reviewed framework has formally derived electromagnetic repulsion as a manifestation of differential attraction to a background gravitational or density field.**

This is the most genuinely novel aspect of GDGM.

Closest related concepts:
- **Gravitoelectromagnetism (GEM):** Formal analogies between GR (weak-field) and Maxwell equations. Not a derivation of EM from gravity.
- **QED Vacuum Screening:** Virtual e⁺e⁻ pairs partially screen bare charge. Physical picture: virtual particles attracted to charge, partially cancelling it. Structurally analogous to "competing attraction" — apparent charge depends on which way nearby fields are being pulled. However, purely quantum, no classical analog.
- **Casimir Effect Repulsion:** Under certain dielectric conditions, Casimir force can be repulsive (Lifshitz 1956). Jaffe (2005) showed Casimir forces can be computed without zero-point energies — they arise from relativistic forces between real charges. Loose structural resonance with GDGM.

GDGM must navigate:
1. Specific mathematical mechanism for like-charge repulsion from density gradient differentials
2. Why mechanism produces exactly 1/r² scaling and U(1) gauge symmetry
3. Why gravitational coupling is 10⁻³⁶ times weaker than electromagnetic coupling

# PART 2 — COSMOLOGY FINDINGS

## 2a. Evidence for Accelerating Expansion

### The 1998 Observations

Two teams found distant Type Ia supernovae 10–15% fainter than expected for matter-dominated deceleration:
- **High-Z Supernova Team** (Riess, Schmidt et al., 1998): 10 SNe Ia, z = 0.16–0.62.
- **Supernova Cosmology Project** (Perlmutter et al., 1999): 42 SNe Ia.

Greater-than-expected faintness → greater distance → faster expansion in the past → accelerating expansion.

### The Interpretation Chain — Embedded Assumptions

Each step from raw data to "accelerating expansion" embeds assumptions:

1. **Type Ia as standard candles:** Not truly standard — "standardizable" via Phillips relation and color. Assumes correlation is universal and doesn't evolve with redshift. If luminosity evolves with host galaxy age/metallicity, distances are systematically wrong.
2. **Distance ladder:** Parallax → Cepheid → SNe Ia. Each step propagates systematic errors. Hubble tension may partially reflect calibration errors.
3. **Homogeneity/isotropy (FLRW metric):** If our local region is underdense (Hubble bubble), we overestimate H₀ and misinterpret expansion history.
4. **Matter content model:** Conversion from flux to "acceleration" requires assuming specific matter/radiation mix. Conclusion of acceleration is robust only within ΛCDM — partially circular.
5. **No intrinsic luminosity variation:** Population drift effects questioned. 2025 analysis found systematic age-related bias in supernova standardization.

**Key Citations:** Riess et al. (1998) AJ 116:1009 arXiv:astro-ph/9805201; Perlmutter et al. (1999) ApJ 517:565.

## 2b. Alternative Interpretations

### Tired Light — Definitively Disfavored

Photons lose energy traveling through space, producing redshift without expansion. Eliminated by:
1. **Time dilation:** Distant supernova light curves time-dilated in proportion to (1+z). Confirmed observationally. Tired light predicts none. Clean, direct test — data match expansion.
2. **Surface brightness (Tolman test):** Expansion predicts (1+z)⁻⁴ dimming; tired light predicts (1+z)⁻³. Observations favor expansion.
3. **CMB spectrum:** Perfect blackbody — predicted by hot Big Bang, cannot be reproduced by tired light.
4. **Image blurring:** Any scattering causing energy loss would blur distant images more than observed.

**Tired light is not viable. GDGM cannot use this mechanism.**

### Timescape Cosmology (Wiltshire) — Most Rigorous Peer-Reviewed Alternative

Apparent acceleration is an artifact of misidentifying local gravitational time dilation with cosmic expansion. Clocks in matter-dense regions (galaxies) tick slower than clocks in voids. Standard FLRW averaging doesn't account for differential clock rates. SNe Ia in/near voids appear at different distances than FLRW predicts, mimicking acceleration.

**2024 results:** Wiltshire et al. published in MNRAS Letters (2024) using full Pantheon+ catalogue (1,535 SNe). Argued for "foundational change to cosmological models." December 2024 Royal Astronomical Society study found dark energy "doesn't exist" in a lumpy universe with backreaction.

**Limitation:** Has not yet produced a full CMB fit comparable to ΛCDM. ESA Euclid satellite (launched 2023) will provide definitive test.

**GDGM alignment opportunity:** If GDGM can formally derive timescape-like phenomena from graviton density gradient dynamics, strong alignment with existing literature. Consider formal correspondence or collaboration with Wiltshire group.

### Void / Hubble Bubble Models

If our galaxy sits inside a local underdensity (~20% less dense, extending ~1 billion light-years), local H₀ appears inflated. 2024 paper: BAO measurements make void model "about one hundred million times more likely than ΛCDM without local void." March 2025 paper (arXiv:2602.03928) proposed "local void model for Hubble and BAO tensions."

### Modified Gravity

- **MOND (Milgrom 1983):** Explains galactic rotation curves without dark matter. Fails at galaxy cluster scale. No relativistic extension works cleanly. GW170817 severely constrained many modified gravity theories (gravitational wave speed = c).
- **f(R) gravity:** Can produce late-time acceleration without Λ. Constrained by solar system tests and GW speed.

### DESI 2024-2025 — Dark Energy May Be Evolving

**Most important recent result for GDGM.**

- DESI DR2 (March 19, 2025): Preference for dynamical dark energy (evolving) over cosmological constant reached **4.2 sigma**.
- Best-fit: w₀ = -0.909 ± 0.081, wₐ = -0.49 (combined DESI + CMB + SNe).

- If this trend continues, dark energy could eventually become attractive, potentially leading to contraction.

## 2c. Dark Energy Problems

### Cosmological Constant Fine-Tuning Problem

Vacuum energy density observed: ~10⁻¹²² in Planck units.
QFT zero-point prediction: ~1 in Planck units.
Discrepancy: **10¹²²** — the largest fine-tuning problem in physics.

Either QFT prediction is wrong (cancels to 120 decimal places by unknown mechanism), the constant is actually zero and something else drives acceleration, or anthropic multiverse selection (unsatisfying). Completely unsolved.

### The Coincidence Problem

Dark matter density (scales as a⁻³) and dark energy density (constant if Λ) are comparable today for no known reason. Requires fine-tuning initial conditions to ~80 decimal places.

### The Hubble Tension (>5 sigma as of 2025)

- CMB (early universe): H₀ = 67.4 ± 0.5 km/s/Mpc
- Local distance ladder (late universe): H₀ = 73.04 ± 1.04 km/s/Mpc

Discrepancy: >5 sigma. Not a statistical fluctuation. Either systematic errors exist or ΛCDM is wrong. January 2025 ScienceDaily: "The universe is expanding too fast to fit theories: Hubble tension in crisis."
