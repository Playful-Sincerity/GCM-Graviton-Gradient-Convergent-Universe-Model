# H2 — Release Theory of Heat: Caveats and Open Problems

**Claim code:** H2
**Date:** 2026-04-23

---

## What This Sketch Establishes

- Structural consistency of the release mechanism with Newton's law of cooling and Fourier heat conduction
- A directional-divergence framing that qualitatively matches the Clausius entropy statement
- A plausible structural analogy between the blackbody spectrum and the distribution of graviton-release states
- A clear comparison to Verlinde's entropic framework (shared substrate-thermodynamic lineage; different substrate and cosmology)

## What This Sketch Does NOT Establish

### UNSPEC-5 (open from v2): Equipartition Not Reproduced

The equipartition theorem ($C_V = \frac{3}{2}kT$ for monatomic ideal gas) is one of the most precisely tested results in classical statistical mechanics. GCM has not derived it. Doing so would require:
- A model of how graviton-release recoil events distribute energy across three spatial degrees of freedom
- A derivation of the Boltzmann factor from graviton microstate statistics
- A formal equivalence between GCM's "temperature = density excess" and Boltzmann's $k_B T$ energy scale

Until this is done, GCM's thermodynamics is at the qualitative level only.

### Planck's Law Not Reproduced

The Planck distribution is the definitive test of quantum thermodynamics. GCM's "distribution of release states" framing is an analogy — it correctly identifies the qualitative structure (peak + high-frequency falloff) but does not reproduce the specific form. Reproducing it would require:
- A density-of-states function for photon-cluster releases
- A derivation of Bose-Einstein statistics (not Maxwell-Boltzmann) from graviton dynamics
- A connection between Planck's constant $h$ and graviton properties

These are red-tier open questions, not yellow-tier sketches.

### The Rate Constant $\lambda$ Is Not Derived

Newton's law of cooling emerges from assuming $\frac{d\rho_g}{dt} \propto -\Delta\rho_g$. GCM does not derive the proportionality constant $\lambda$ from graviton properties. This would require modeling photon-cluster emission rates as a function of local density packing — which requires a specific model of particle structure that GCM does not yet have.

### The Density-Temperature Mapping Is Asserted, Not Derived

The whole argument rests on the mapping $T \propto \rho_g - \rho_0$. This is plausible (hotter means more disequilibrium means higher local density), but GCM has not derived this proportionality from first principles. A complete derivation would need to show that the mapping preserves the thermometric properties of temperature (zeroth law, additivity, etc.).

### Second Law Is Not Derived

GCM's directional divergence principle produces the Clausius statement (heat flows from hot to cold), but it does not derive the full Second Law. In particular, GCM has not shown that entropy (in the thermodynamic sense) is non-decreasing, or that it can be identified with a function of graviton microstate counts. Boltzmann's $S = k_B \ln W$ has no GCM counterpart yet.

## How This Becomes Green

For H2 to achieve green tier, GCM would need at least one of:
1. Derivation of equipartition from graviton-release statistics
2. Derivation of the density-of-states for graviton-release events, showing it reproduces Planck's law to leading order
3. Formal derivation of the density-temperature mapping from first principles

Any of these would constitute a significant theoretical advance, not a short afternoon's work. H2 is expected to remain yellow-tier for the current planning horizon (Sessions A–J).

## Comparison to Existing Physics

The release-of-photon-clusters mechanism is structurally related to:
- **Thermal radiation in quantum electrodynamics** — both attribute heat to photon emission. QED derives the Planck distribution exactly; GCM has not.
- **Verlinde entropic gravity** — both treat thermodynamics as substrate-level. Neither has reproduced equipartition from their respective substrates.
- **Padmanabhan emergent thermodynamics** — also treats gravity as thermodynamically fundamental; similar open questions about equipartition and Planck's law.

GCM is not alone in having these gaps. But the gaps are real, and naming them is the honesty standard the program commits to.
