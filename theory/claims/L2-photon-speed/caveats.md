# L2 — Caveats

## What This Proves

- **Dimensional and algebraic consistency.** The standard SR formula $v = c \sqrt{1 - (m_0 c^2 / E)^2}$ applied with GCM's photon-mass hypothesis yields a numerically precise $\Delta v$ that is consistent with special relativity.
- **Compatibility with current observational bounds.** GCM's photon-mass value $\sim 10^{-54}$ kg is ~3.6 orders of magnitude below the tightest current bound (Wang et al. 2023 FRB dispersion, $2.1 \times 10^{-15}$ eV/$c^2$).
- **Smooth reduction to the massless case.** As $m_0 \to 0$, $v \to c$ exactly.

## What This Does Not Prove

- **Not a derivation of the photon rest mass.** $m_0 \approx 10^{-54}$ kg is placed by hand — chosen because it is safely below current bounds. No GCM-internal derivation of this value exists.
- **Not a prediction of a new effect.** $\Delta v / c \sim 10^{-37}$ is many orders below any experimental sensitivity. L2 cannot be empirically distinguished from the standard massless-photon case.
- **Not a resolution of the photon-as-graviton-structure question.** GCM claims the photon is a traveling structure of gravitons, which would have further testable implications (e.g. energy-dependent dispersion from internal structure). Those are L3 and R6 — separate claims, not established by L2.

## Key Tension — The Photon-Graviton Mass Hierarchy

GCM's proposed masses:
- $m_g \approx 4 \times 10^{-87}$ kg (M1)
- $m_\gamma \approx 10^{-54}$ kg (L2 input)

Ratio: $m_\gamma / m_g \approx 10^{33}$. A photon is therefore ~$10^{33}$ gravitons in GCM's reading. For an optical photon (energy $\sim 1$ eV, $m_\gamma c^2 \sim 10^{-37}$ J), the *effective* mass from $E/c^2$ is $\sim 10^{-36}$ kg — only $\sim 10^{51}$ gravitons by the direct energy-mass conversion. The discrepancy (the photon's rest mass being $\sim 10^{-54}$ kg while its effective energy-mass is $\sim 10^{-36}$ kg) is acceptable — rest mass and relativistic mass are different quantities — but the "photon as graviton structure" reading needs to connect these at some level. This is L3 territory and not resolved here.

## Honesty Flags for the Coherence Paper

1. **Do not present L2 as a test of GCM.** It is a *compatibility check*, not a test. Distinguishing-test claims belong to R6 (time dilation of FRB signals in redshift) and L3 (wavelength-spacing argument) — both aspirational.
2. **Do not imply the photon is definitively massive.** The standard-model massless photon remains the default; L2 shows GCM is compatible with the narrow slice of parameter space in which the photon has a small mass.
3. **Be precise about the FRB bound's status.** Wang et al. 2023 is a bound from dispersion of FRB signals; it does not measure a non-zero mass, only constrains it from above.

## Interaction with R6 (Photon-Structure Time Dilation)

R6 is GCM's proposed mechanism for $(1 + z)^{1.003}$ time dilation in SNe Ia light curves. The argument cascades: photon wavelength $\propto$ (1+z) → internal graviton cluster spacing $\propto$ wavelength → information delay $\propto$ spacing. R6 presupposes a non-trivial internal structure for the photon. L2's small rest mass is consistent with that structure but does not derive it. If R6 fails (cannot produce the $(1+z)^{1.003}$ scaling), L2 still stands — it is formally independent.

## Open Questions Surfaced

- **Why $m_\gamma \approx 10^{-54}$ kg specifically?** The v2 document treats this as a placeholder; no derivation exists. Could just as well be $10^{-55}$ or $10^{-53}$ and pass the FRB bound.
- **Does GCM's photon-structure reading produce energy-dependent dispersion?** The FRB bound assumes a *simple* massive-photon dispersion law ($v \propto \sqrt{1 - (m_0 c^2/E)^2}$). GCM's more complex structure might introduce further $E$-dependence. Deferred to L3/R6.
- **How does one reconcile a massive photon with the precise constraints from gauge invariance?** A Proca-field massive photon breaks $U(1)$ gauge symmetry softly; the standard-model literature (Goldhaber & Nieto 2010 for the canonical review) treats this carefully. GCM's treatment is not yet at that level.

## Relationship to Other Claims

- **Independent of M1, E7, Q3, M3** at the formula level.
- **L1 (photon mass vs FRB bound)** — L2 uses L1's input value.
- **L3 (wavelength ∝ graviton-cluster spacing)** — aspirational sister claim; L3 would justify why the photon has internal structure at all.
- **R6 (photon-structure time dilation)** — downstream claim depending on photon-as-structure. L2 does not depend on R6, but R6 depends on L2 being consistent.
