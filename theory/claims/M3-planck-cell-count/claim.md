# M3 — Universe Planck-Cell Count

**Claim code:** M3
**Epistemic tier:** Green (4/4 verification checks pass).
**Section:** S2 — Verifiable Math Spine
**Source:** Standard cosmological arithmetic applied to GCM's "one graviton per Planck volume" reading.
**Verified:** 2026-04-23

## The Claim

Treating the observable universe as a sphere of radius $R_H \approx 4.4 \times 10^{26}$ m (Hubble radius) and assigning one graviton per Planck volume $\ell_P^3$:

$$N = \frac{(4/3) \pi R_H^3}{\ell_P^3} \approx 8.5 \times 10^{184} \approx 10^{185}$$

This is the estimated total number of gravitons in the observable universe if space is Planck-quantized and uniformly packed at one graviton per Planck cell.

## Framing

M3 is a **dimensional estimate**, not a measurement. It establishes the order-of-magnitude scale of "degrees of freedom" GCM's one-graviton-per-Planck-volume assumption implies. The figure is useful as (a) a sanity check against other cosmological counts (baryons, holographic bound), and (b) a scale for downstream claims such as "how many gravitons participate in a single particle."

---

## What This Claim Is

- An arithmetic combination of three well-known numbers: $R_H$ (Hubble radius), $\ell_P$ (Planck length), and the sphere-volume formula.
- A GCM-specific *interpretation*: this number is the "graviton count of the observable universe" under the one-graviton-per-$\ell_P^3$ assumption.

## What This Claim Is Not

- **Not a prediction.** The number cannot be measured.
- **Not a derivation of $\rho_0$ (the vacuum graviton density).** $\rho_0$ is open (OQ-4). M3 assumes uniform Planck-scale packing as a dimensional reference; the actual vacuum density is unknown.
- **Not a claim about the geometry of the universe.** The sphere-volume formula assumes Euclidean geometry over the Hubble radius; relativistic corrections for curvature and finite-age effects are ignored in this dimensional estimate.
- **Not a claim that space is granular at Planck scale.** The Planck length is the natural scale for quantum gravity but its role as "graviton grain size" is a GCM-specific reading.
