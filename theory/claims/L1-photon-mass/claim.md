# L1 — Photon Has Small Rest Mass

**Section:** S5 (Light and Photon Physics)
**Claim code:** L1
**Epistemic tier:** 🟡 Yellow — dimensionally consistent, within all known bounds, but not derived from first principles. A massive photon is possible within GCM's ontology; the specific value is an estimate.
**Session:** E
**Date:** 2026-04-23

---

## The Claim

The photon has a small but non-zero rest mass:

$$m_\gamma \approx 10^{-54} \text{ kg} \approx 5.61 \times 10^{-19} \text{ eV}/c^2$$

This is consistent with all published photon-mass bounds, including the tightest FRB dispersion constraint from Wang et al. (2023): $m_\gamma \leq 2.1 \times 10^{-15}$ eV/c².

---

## GCM Motivation

In GCM's ontology, a photon is a traveling density structure of gravitons — a localized, moving configuration of the graviton substrate. A graviton has mass $m_g$. A photon composed of $\xi$ gravitons has mass $m_\gamma = m_g \cdot \xi > 0$. There is no mechanism in GCM that would produce a perfectly massless photon; the standard model's massless photon emerges from gauge symmetry (U(1) invariance), which GCM does not adopt as fundamental.

The specific value $10^{-54}$ kg is a rough estimate, not a derivation. It reflects the assumption that a photon contains very few gravitons relative to massive particles. It is consistent with the constraint that the photon must travel at speeds indistinguishable from $c$ in all current experiments.

---

## What This Claim Does and Does Not Prove

**Does:** Show that a photon rest mass of $\sim 10^{-54}$ kg is consistent with all known experimental bounds as of 2026. Show the unit conversion. Flag the margin against the Wang 2023 bound.

**Does not:** Derive the value of $m_\gamma$ from GCM's framework. Claim this is the measured photon mass. Reproduce the specific mass spectrum of photons (GCM has no prediction for why photons should be this light rather than some other value).
