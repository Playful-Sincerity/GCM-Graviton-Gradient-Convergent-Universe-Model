# L3 — Caveats

**Claim code:** L3

---

## Caveat 1 — This Is Reframing, Not Derivation

L3 does not derive the photon wave equation from graviton dynamics. It identifies $\lambda$ with $N \cdot d$ where $d$ is a postulated graviton-cluster spacing. The identification is dimensionally consistent and compatible with standard electromagnetic phenomenology, but it does not *predict* the wave structure — it re-describes it.

A green-tier version of L3 would show that gravitons in a traveling density configuration *must* form periodic density peaks, and compute $N$ from first principles.

---

## Caveat 2 — $N$ Is Undetermined

The constant $N$ (clusters per wavelength) is not specified. Without a mechanism fixing $N$, the cluster spacing $d$ is determined only up to this integer factor.

$N = 1$ (one cluster per wavelength, with $d = \lambda$) is the simplest case. But values like $N = 2$ (two density peaks per cycle, analogous to intensity oscillating at twice the field frequency) or $N \gg 1$ are also structurally possible.

**For R6, $N$ cancels.** The scaling $d_{\text{obs}}/d_{\text{emit}} = (1+z)$ holds regardless of $N$'s specific value, provided $N$ is constant across emission and observation. So R6's qualitative argument does not depend on resolving this caveat.

But a quantitative GCM prediction for any wavelength-specific phenomenon (Lamb shift at the graviton-cluster scale, photon self-interference at scales comparable to $d$, anomalous scattering off clusters) would require $N$.

---

## Caveat 3 — Polarization Is Unaddressed

Photons have two polarization states (for a massless photon) or three (for a massive photon). L3 says nothing about how these states are encoded in graviton-cluster structure. Proposals:

- **Orientation of clusters in 3D:** Clusters have shape (not spherically symmetric); polarization is the cluster's orientation.
- **Handedness of cluster sequence:** The order or chirality of successive clusters encodes circular polarization.

Neither has been developed. L3 is currently silent on polarization — this is a yellow-tier limitation.

---

## Caveat 4 — Relationship to Maxwell's Equations

Standard electrodynamics is encoded in Maxwell's equations. GCM must eventually recover these in the appropriate limit. L3 does not do this — it assumes the wavelength $\lambda$ exists as a recognizable quantity and identifies it with $N \cdot d$. The derivation of $\lambda$ itself — the mechanism by which graviton density structures have a well-defined periodicity — is an open research problem.

A structural concern: Maxwell's equations produce photons as helicity-1 states of a massless gauge field. GCM's photon is a helicity-ambiguous density structure of a massive graviton substrate. Whether GCM can recover the gauge-theoretic structure of electrodynamics in a limit is an open challenge (related to the broader question of how GCM reproduces the standard model).

---

## Caveat 5 — No Prediction From L3 Alone

L3 makes no standalone, testable prediction. Its entire payoff is in enabling R6 (photon-structure time dilation). If R6's argument fails for some other reason (e.g., if the information-delay interpretation is wrong), L3 does no work — it becomes a reframing with no experimental consequence.

**Falsification of L3:** If experiments ever show that a photon's internal structure does NOT scale with its wavelength — for instance, if photon-photon interactions reveal a fixed internal length scale independent of wavelength — L3 would be falsified. Current experimental sensitivity is far from this regime.

---

## What Would Promote L3 to Green

1. A derivation (from some candidate GCM wave equation) that $N$ equals a specific integer (e.g., $N = 2$ for intensity-frequency doubling).
2. A mechanism for polarization in terms of graviton-cluster orientation.
3. Recovery of Maxwell's equations in the low-graviton-density, long-wavelength limit.

Any one of these would substantially harden L3. As of this writing, none exist.
