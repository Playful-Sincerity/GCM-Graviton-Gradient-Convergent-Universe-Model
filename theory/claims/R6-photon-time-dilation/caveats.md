# R6 — Caveats

**Claim code:** R6

---

## Caveat 1 — Dependence on L3

R6 is a consequence of L3 plus the information-propagation premise. If L3 fails — if the photon's internal cluster spacing does NOT scale linearly with wavelength (for instance, if the structural parameter $N$ depends on wavelength) — R6 loses its mechanism.

**Current status of L3:** Yellow-tier. A reframing that is dimensionally consistent and compatible with standard electromagnetic phenomenology, but not derived from first principles.

**Inherited risk:** R6 inherits L3's provisional status. A failure of L3 would require GCM's cosmology to rely on alternative time-dilation mechanisms (Timescape-type clock-rate structure, or a not-yet-specified GCM mechanism).

---

## Caveat 2 — Information-Propagation Premise

R6 assumes that information encoded in the photon's structure propagates between clusters at the photon speed $v$ (with $v < c$ strictly; $c$ is the causality limit, not a speed that any composite thing achieves — see `theory/speed-of-causality-principle.md`). This is not a derivation; it is an assertion that successive clusters arrive at the observer at the intervals implied by their spatial separation and common propagation speed.

**What could go wrong:**
- If graviton clusters within a photon interact with each other in ways that cause them to "bunch" or "spread" during propagation (beyond the initial redshift stretching), the spacing at observation would not simply be $(1+z)$ times the spacing at emission.
- If information is not carried by successive cluster arrivals — for example, if photon-borne information is encoded holographically across the entire cluster structure — the $\Delta t = d/c$ step may not apply.

Neither possibility is obviously excluded by current physics; R6's simplicity may oversimplify photon dynamics in a fully worked GCM.

---

## Caveat 3 — The Exact Exponent

R6 predicts exactly $(1+z)^{1.000}$ time dilation. DES 2024 measures $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$. These are consistent at better than 1σ, but they are not identical central values.

**If future measurements tighten the DES result to $\alpha = 1.005 \pm 0.001$** (a plausible improvement with the Legacy Survey of Space and Time / LSST), then R6's prediction of $\alpha = 1.000$ would be excluded at 5σ. This is a genuine falsification risk.

**Possible resolutions if this occurs:**
- A more detailed GCM treatment (specifying graviton-cluster dynamics, propagation effects, or the relationship between cluster count and wavelength) might produce a small deviation from linear scaling.
- The deviation might track a specific cosmological parameter that GCM can derive — turning the apparent discrepancy into a prediction.
- Alternatively, R6 may be correct at leading order but incomplete; the full treatment produces corrections that match observation.

As of this writing, current data does not push R6 into falsification. Future precision could.

---

## Caveat 4 — Equivalence to Observed Cosmological Time Dilation

SNe Ia light-curve time dilation is one observed flavor of cosmological time dilation. Other tests exist (GRB durations, quasar variability timescales). R6 should produce $(1+z)$ dilation for all of these, not just SNe Ia. The coherence paper should note this and flag any observational test where R6 would predict something different (none currently expected, but worth verifying for GRBs and quasar variability).

---

## Caveat 5 — Not a Derivation From Graviton Dynamics

A green-tier R6 would derive the time-dilation result from a graviton-dynamical model of the photon. This would require:
1. A specific equation of motion for gravitons within a photon-like density structure.
2. A demonstration that this structure's internal periodicity is preserved during propagation (modulo the redshift stretching).
3. A computation showing information propagation between clusters occurs at the photon speed $v$ (where $v = c - 0_x$; see speed-of-causality principle).

None of these exist. R6 is a consistency argument: **given** L3's wavelength-spacing identification and **given** information travels at the photon speed $v$, time dilation scales as $(1+z)$. The "given"s are not derived from GCM; they are motivated by the broader GCM ontology but treated as premises here.

---

## Caveat 6 — No Quantitative Distinction From Timescape Via This Test Alone

R6 is consistent with DES 2024. So is Timescape. So is standard ΛCDM (which predicts exactly $(1+z)^1$ time dilation from metric expansion). The DES 2024 result alone cannot distinguish between these three.

R6's value is that it shows GCM **can** produce the required time dilation — removing a potential falsification. It does not uniquely confirm GCM. To move from "R6 is consistent" to "R6 is preferred," additional distinguishing tests would be needed (e.g., precision measurements at high $z$ that distinguish $\alpha = 1.000$ from $\alpha = 1.005$, or direct tests of photon internal structure).

---

## What Would Falsify R6 (Clearly)

- A precise measurement excluding $\alpha = 1.000$ at > 3σ — e.g., $\alpha = 1.010 \pm 0.002$ would be a 5σ exclusion of R6.
- Experimental demonstration that photon internal structure does NOT scale with wavelength — undermining L3 and therefore R6.
- A theoretical result showing that information in a periodic density structure does not propagate at the photon speed $v$ between structural elements.

## What Would Promote R6 to Green

1. A quantitative GCM derivation of the photon as a specific graviton-cluster configuration.
2. A first-principles computation of the time-dilation factor from graviton dynamics, reproducing $(1+z)^1$ without assuming L3 and the information-propagation premise as inputs.
3. A testable prediction distinguishing R6 from other $(1+z)$-time-dilation mechanisms.
