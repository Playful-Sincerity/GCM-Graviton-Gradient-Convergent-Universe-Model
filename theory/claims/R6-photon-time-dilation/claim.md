## The Claim

A cosmologically redshifted photon carries information — phase, modulation, wave-train identity — via its internal graviton-cluster structure. As the photon redshifts, its internal cluster spacing (per L3) increases by the factor $(1+z)$. Information carried by the photon's structure is communicated at the photon speed $v$ (with $v < c$; see `theory/speed-of-causality-principle.md`) between successive clusters. Therefore the time between successive information arrivals at the observer scales linearly with $(1+z)$:

$$\frac{\Delta t_{\text{obs}}}{\Delta t_{\text{emit}}} = (1+z)$$

This is **time dilation of the photon's information stream**, produced as a direct geometric consequence of the wavelength-spacing relation in L3. It is not a new assumption; it follows from L3 plus the premise that information in a periodic density structure is communicated by successive density peaks.

**Critical empirical target:** DES 2024 (arXiv:2406.05050) measured SNe Ia light-curve time dilation at $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$ with ~200σ significance. R6 produces exactly $(1+z)^{1.000}$, which falls within the 1σ error bars of the DES measurement ($1.003 \pm 0.005 \supset 1.000$).

---

## Why This Is the Critical Claim

Classical tired light — the hypothesis that photons lose energy to some friction or scattering as they traverse the universe — was falsified because it produces redshift without time dilation. A "tired" photon would have lower energy but the same wave-train duration as at emission; distant SNe Ia light curves would not appear stretched. DES 2024 confirmed at ~200σ that high-redshift supernovae **do** show time-dilated light curves, matching a nearly-linear $(1+z)$ scaling.

GCM must produce this time dilation structurally — not by invoking metric expansion (which GCM does not posit) and not by accident. R6 is GCM's mechanism.

**If R6 fails, GCM's cosmology fails.** Without a mechanism for $(1+z)$ time dilation, GCM's redshift-without-expansion story becomes indistinguishable from tired light and is excluded by the same data.

## What R6 Is and Is Not

**R6 is:**
- A structural argument that follows $(1+z)$ time dilation from L3's wavelength-spacing identification plus the premise that photon-borne information travels at the photon speed $v$ ($v < c$, per the speed-of-causality principle) between internal clusters.
- Consistent with the DES 2024 measurement of $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$ (since $(1+z)^{1.000}$ is within error).
- Independent of Timescape's clock-rate mechanism; both could operate.

**R6 is not:**
- A full derivation of the DES exponent $1.003$. R6 gives exactly 1.000; the $0.003$ is within experimental uncertainty and cannot be distinguished by current data.
- A derivation from first-principles graviton dynamics. R6 relies on L3's identification and an information-propagation premise.
- A redshift mechanism itself. R6 is the **time-dilation companion** to whatever redshift mechanism GCM commits to in S6 (R1–R5 are the candidates). Any redshift mechanism that stretches $\lambda$ by $(1+z)$ will, via L3, stretch $d$ by $(1+z)$, and R6 follows.

## Structural Consequence

Because R6 is a geometric consequence of L3, **any redshift mechanism GCM adopts** — whether space friction (R1), void decompression (R2), selection-effect (R5), or a hybrid — inherits the correct time dilation automatically, provided the mechanism stretches the photon's wavelength and the photon's internal structure scales with wavelength.

This means GCM's cosmology is not crippled by the tired-light falsification. Any GCM redshift mechanism that operates on wavelength operates on cluster spacing too, and therefore produces time dilation.

**This is the load-bearing structural feature of GCM's response to the DES 2024 constraint.**

- **L3** (wavelength-spacing identification) — R6 directly depends on L3. If L3 fails, R6 loses its mechanism.
- **R1–R5** (candidate redshift mechanisms in S6) — R6 is the time-dilation companion for any of these. It does not itself specify *why* wavelength increases with distance; it specifies what follows from that increase.
