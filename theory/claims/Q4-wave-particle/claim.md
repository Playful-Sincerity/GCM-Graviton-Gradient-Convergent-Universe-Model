## The Conceptual Claim

In GCM, what standard quantum mechanics calls a "probability amplitude" $\psi(\mathbf{x}, t)$ is, ontologically, a **projection onto a real distribution of graviton density**. The wave function is not a catalogue of potential-outcome probabilities; it is a mathematical encoding of an actually existing, spatially extended graviton structure.

Wave-particle duality is therefore not epistemic (a reflection of our ignorance) nor a superposition of classically distinct states; it is the natural appearance of an extended density gradient with a sharp center and a gradual, real boundary.

Measurement-apparent stochasticity — the Born rule probabilities seen in actual experiments — reflects our ignorance of the full graviton microstate, not a fundamental indeterminism in nature. This is a commitment to **realism**, in direct opposition to the Copenhagen instrumentalist reading.

---

## The Three Commitments

GCM's reading of QM requires three simultaneous commitments:

1. **Realism.** Graviton density distributions are real physical structures, not mathematical bookkeeping for measurement probabilities. $\psi$ projects onto something actually there.
2. **Locality.** No faster-than-light signals, no action at a distance beyond what propagation through the graviton substrate permits. Per Wisdom's 2026-04-23 formulation: *"Local realism. Not non-local."*
3. **Superdeterminism.** The assumption of *measurement independence* in Bell's theorem is denied. In a fully deterministic universe, the hidden variables of quantum particles and the settings chosen by experimenters are both consequences of the same prior conditions. Their correlations reflect common history, not nonlocal signaling.

These three commitments together select the **local-realist path via superdeterminism**. This is a minority position in foundations of physics, but a coherent one — and crucially, the one that preserves both locality and realism in the face of Bell-inequality experiments.

---

## Why the Superdeterministic Qualifier Is Load-Bearing

This is the section a physicist audience will scan most aggressively. "Local realism" without immediate qualification is a phrase that gets dismissed within seconds by anyone Bell-literate: Bell's theorem rules out local hidden variables (under standard assumptions). A paper claiming "local realism" and leaving it at that is classified as naïve and set aside.

**The qualification matters because:**

- Bell's theorem has an *assumption*: measurement independence, also called statistical independence or free-choice. Formally: $P(\lambda | a, b) = P(\lambda)$, where $\lambda$ is the hidden-variable state and $a, b$ are the measurement settings. This says the hidden variables are uncorrelated with the setting choices.
- **Superdeterminism denies this assumption.** In a fully deterministic universe, $\lambda$, $a$, and $b$ all descend from the same initial conditions. Their joint distribution need not factorize as Bell assumes.
- **Therefore** a superdeterministic theory can preserve locality *and* realism while still violating Bell inequalities — the violation is explained as a correlation of common origin, not as nonlocal influence.

A physicist reader who sees "local, realist, superdeterministic — following 't Hooft (2016)" will recognize the position as a defensible (if contrarian) interpretation. A physicist reader who sees only "local realism" will dismiss it as a student-level mistake. The qualifier is the difference.

## Nearest-Neighbor Precedent

This position is not novel with GCM. The nearest-neighbor precedent is:

- **Gerard 't Hooft, *The Cellular Automaton Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics*** (Springer, 2016). 't Hooft (Nobel laureate, 1999) argues that quantum mechanics is the statistical mechanics of an underlying deterministic cellular automaton. The book is the most developed superdeterministic-realist program.
- **Sabine Hossenfelder**, numerous essays 2020–2024, has publicly defended superdeterminism as an underdiscussed but legitimate position in foundations.
- **John S. Bell himself** acknowledged superdeterminism as a logical escape from his theorem (calling it "absolute determinism"), although he considered it philosophically unattractive.

This is a minority view, not a crackpot one. The GCM coherence paper should cite 't Hooft and note that the view is contested — *and* defensible.

---

## What Superdeterminism Costs

Superdeterminism denies **free choice** in the measurement-setting sense. An experimenter's decision to measure spin along axis $a$ rather than axis $b$ is, under superdeterminism, not genuinely independent of the particle's hidden state — both are consequences of the same deterministic flow from earlier conditions.

Many readers find this philosophically uncomfortable. Three responses, in increasing order of strength:

1. **Pragmatic:** "Free choice" in physics is a technical assumption, not a metaphysical claim. Denying the assumption does not deny phenomenological free will in any ordinary sense.
2. **Empirical:** No experiment has ever verified measurement independence directly. It is assumed, not tested. Superdeterminism is not refuted by any observation.
3. **Consonance with GCM's ethos:** GCM's broader framing — the universe as a converging, deterministic gravitational flow — is already committed to a deeper determinism. Free choice as a local illusion over a fully deterministic substrate is consonant with the ethos, not an added cost.

The paper should acknowledge the discomfort honestly and proceed.

## What a Full Treatment Would Require (and Why This Is Out of Scope)

A full wave-particle-as-real-distribution treatment would reformulate quantum mechanics in terms of graviton-substrate dynamics. That program would need to:

1. **Derive the Schrödinger equation** (or something equivalent) from graviton microdynamics. Today there is no such derivation; the minimal-model equation of motion is purely classical Newtonian gravity.
2. **Recover the Born rule** as a statistical-mechanics result (probability proportional to microstate count), in the spirit of 't Hooft's cellular-automaton-to-QM mapping.
3. **Explain double-slit interference** as constructive/destructive overlap of real graviton density distributions, not amplitude superposition. The source notes (gcm_v2.md §"Wave-particle duality") gesture at this: "Double-slit interference occurs because the outer levels of a photon's density distribution interact with both slits simultaneously. Measurement collapses this by absorbing the outer levels." This is a qualitative sketch, not a derivation.
4. **Address entanglement.** Two-particle correlations in Bell-violating experiments must follow from correlated initial graviton configurations, not from nonlocal connection. This is where superdeterminism does the real work.
5. **Reproduce QFT in some limit.** How does the Standard Model emerge from graviton dynamics? Red-tier (S8 structural challenge).

None of these is accomplished in the coherence paper. Q4 is a **conceptual commitment**, not a program-of-work. The paper's job is to state the commitment clearly, cite the 't Hooft precedent, and flag the structural challenge as future work.
