# Q4 — Caveats

---

## 1. No numerical verification

Q4 is a conceptual/interpretive claim. There is no formula to dimensionally analyze, no number to cross-check against a published bound, no derivation to verify step by step. This is the correct tier call: a numerical "verification" of an interpretive commitment would be a category error.

**What to say in the coherence paper:** "Q4 is a conceptual commitment. Numerical verification is not applicable at this tier."

---

## 2. Superdeterminism is a minority view

Most working physicists do not adopt superdeterminism. The dominant interpretations of QM are Copenhagen (instrumentalist), Many-Worlds (Everett), and nonlocal hidden variables (Bohmian mechanics). Superdeterminism has a small but serious community (Hooft, Hossenfelder) and a long intellectual pedigree back to Bell himself.

**Implication:** A physicist reader sympathetic to realism will often expect Bohmian mechanics as GCM's Bell escape. GCM explicitly declines that route (per Wisdom's 2026-04-23 commitment to locality, which Bohmian mechanics violates). The paper must make this choice visible, not quietly assume it.

---

## 3. No derivation of the Schrödinger equation

GCM does not derive the Schrödinger equation or its relativistic generalizations from graviton dynamics. The minimal-model equation of motion is purely classical Newtonian gravity applied to $\sim 10^{185}$ gravitons. A genuine derivation of QM from graviton dynamics — in the spirit of 't Hooft's CA → QM mapping — is a multi-decade research program, not a coherence-paper deliverable.

**What this means:** When the paper says "wave function as projection onto a real distribution," the projection operation is not defined mathematically. It is a conceptual commitment about what the wave function *refers to*, not a computational procedure.

---

## 4. Entanglement is not addressed mechanistically

Q4 commits to explaining Bell-violating correlations via superdeterminism (common history of the entangled particles and the measurement settings). But GCM has no explicit calculation of any such correlation. The Bell-violation phenomena are left as a direction the mechanism would explain, not as a phenomenon the mechanism *does* explain.

This is the largest single gap inside Q4. Without at least a toy model of an entangled pair with explicitly correlated initial conditions producing the observed correlations, Q4's superdeterministic commitment remains a philosophical move rather than a physical one.

---

## 5. Double-slit interference is sketched, not derived

The gcm_v2.md language — "outer levels of a photon's density distribution interact with both slits simultaneously; measurement collapses this by absorbing the outer levels" — is a qualitative picture, not a derivation. A full treatment would need to:

- Define what "level" of a density distribution means mathematically.
- Show that the overlap of density distributions at the detector screen reproduces $|\psi|^2$ interference pattern.
- Explain why the interference vanishes when which-path information is available (a real phenomenon, not just a Copenhagen interpretive quirk).

None of this is done. The sketch is a useful orientation; it is not an argument.

---

## 6. Relationship to CC-5 (Axiom of Indivisibility)

Q4 is downstream of CC-5. The Axiom forbids the graviton from possessing intrinsic quantum properties (spin, phase, internal state). Therefore whatever gives rise to quantum behavior must be *configurational* — properties of the distribution, not of its constituents.

This puts GCM in a specific corner of the interpretive landscape:

- Not Copenhagen (which doesn't need a realist substrate).
- Not Many-Worlds (which accepts amplitude superposition as fundamental).
- Not Bohmian (which violates locality).
- Superdeterministic-realist, with graviton distributions as the hidden-variable substrate.

This is 't Hooft's corner. GCM is a substrate-specific version of the 't Hooft program.

---

## 7. What Q4 contributes to the coherence case

Q4 is the quantum-mechanics piece of CC-6's "balance across physics domains" requirement. Even as a red-tier conceptual claim, it:

- States GCM's interpretive commitment clearly.
- Places GCM in a recognized position on the foundations-of-QM map.
- Names the structural work that remains (derive Schrödinger, derive Born rule, explain double-slit, explain entanglement).
- Cites real precedent (t Hooft 2016).

For a coherence case, this is sufficient. For a theory claim, it is not — and the paper should say so.

---

## 8. What would upgrade Q4

Ordered from easier to harder:

1. **Small:** A worked example of a single-particle graviton density distribution that reproduces a specific QM phenomenon (e.g., the Gaussian ground state of a harmonic oscillator) qualitatively.
2. **Medium:** A toy model of an entangled pair with correlated initial conditions producing a specific Bell-violating correlation.
3. **Large:** A derivation-sketch of the Schrödinger equation from graviton statistical mechanics, in the spirit of 't Hooft.
4. **Full:** A reformulation of QM in terms of graviton-substrate dynamics, reproducing the standard predictions in a well-defined limit.

Steps 1–2 might be accessible in a focused research push. Steps 3–4 are multi-year programs. None of this is in scope for the coherence paper.

---

## 9. Risk of being dismissed despite the qualification

Even with the superdeterministic qualifier, some physicist readers will dismiss the position on sight — superdeterminism is unpopular enough that shallow readings will class it with crackpot physics. Mitigation:

- Cite 't Hooft prominently. His Nobel and the published CA interpretation do substantial credibility work.
- Acknowledge the position is minority; don't oversell its acceptance.
- Don't stake the entire coherence case on Q4. Q4 is one piece of S4; the paper's credibility also rests on Q2 (green), Q3 (green), and the S3 unification work.

The risk-managed play is: state Q4 cleanly, cite the precedent, flag the cost, move on. Don't let Q4 become the center of gravity of the paper.
