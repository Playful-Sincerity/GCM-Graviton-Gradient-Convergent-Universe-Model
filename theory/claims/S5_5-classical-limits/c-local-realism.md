## The Claim

GCM commits to **realism**, **locality**, and **determinism**. Bell-violating correlations observed in quantum experiments (Aspect 1982, Hensen 2015, BIG Bell Test 2018) are explained as **common-history artifacts** in a fully deterministic graviton flow — not as faster-than-light signals, not as fundamental indeterminism.

The non-standard move is denying **measurement independence**: the assumption that experimenters' choices of measurement settings are uncorrelated with the hidden variables of the measured particles. In a fully deterministic universe, measurement choices and particle states are both consequences of the same prior conditions; their correlations reflect shared causal history.

This position is called **superdeterminism**. GCM's commitment to it is load-bearing for the coherence case because Bell's theorem otherwise forces GCM to abandon either realism or locality — both of which GCM insists on.

---

## Why This Framing Is Load-Bearing

When the coherence paper claims "local realism" without qualification, a working physicist will dismiss it within seconds on Bell-inequality grounds. Any defensible local-realist position in 2026 must address Bell. GCM's answer is superdeterminism, and it **must be named explicitly** — not treated as a technicality, not tucked into a footnote.

**Required phrasing for the paper:**

> GCM commits to real graviton density distributions (realism) and to local propagation of effects at or below $c$ (locality), in a fully deterministic substrate. Bell-violating correlations are explained as common-history correlations in this deterministic flow, not as nonlocal signals. This is a superdeterministic reading of quantum correlations, following 't Hooft (2016) *Cellular Automaton Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics*. Measurement independence — the assumption that experimenter choices are uncorrelated with particle hidden variables — is what GCM denies.

Without this phrasing, "local realism" reads as Bell-naïve. With this phrasing, it reads as a specific, defensible, minority position with peer-reviewed precedent.

## The Bell-Theorem Landscape (For the Physicist Audience)

Bell's theorem (1964) shows that any theory that is simultaneously:
1. **Local** — no faster-than-light signals
2. **Realist** — definite physical states exist independent of measurement
3. **Obeying measurement independence** — experimenter choices uncorrelated with hidden variables

cannot reproduce the correlations predicted by quantum mechanics and observed in experiment.

The three escape hatches are therefore:
- **Deny locality** → pilot-wave theories (Bohmian mechanics), many-worlds without a preferred basis
- **Deny realism** → Copenhagen interpretation, QBism, consistent histories
- **Deny measurement independence** → superdeterminism (**GCM's choice**)

Each has costs. Denying locality contradicts SR's spirit. Denying realism makes the question "what exists before measurement?" unanswerable. Denying measurement independence is philosophically uncomfortable (it denies a libertarian reading of free will) but has no experimental conflict. 't Hooft, Hossenfelder, and others have argued it is the least costly of the three.

**GCM's commitment:** measurement independence is what goes. Locality and realism are preserved.

## Why Superdeterminism Fits GCM Naturally

GCM's minimal-model equation is purely deterministic:
$$\ddot{\mathbf{x}}_i = \sum_{j\neq i} \frac{G m_g}{r_{ij}^2}\,\hat{r}_{ij}$$

Given all graviton positions and velocities at some initial time $t_0$, every subsequent state is fixed. There is no built-in indeterminism, no stochastic term, no measurement-triggered collapse.

In this framework:
- An experimenter at time $t_1$ choosing "measure spin along $\hat{z}$" is a specific configuration of graviton positions and velocities.
- An entangled pair created at time $t_0 < t_1$ is another specific configuration.
- Both configurations trace back to some prior state (the initial condition of the universe, or some earlier shared past light cone).
- Correlations between the measurement choice and the pair's hidden variables are **built in** by the shared history.

No FTL signaling is needed to explain Bell violations. The correlations are there because the universe is a single deterministic flow; measurement setup and particle state are not independent because they share a causal past.

**This is not an ad-hoc rescue.** It is the straightforward reading of Bell correlations in any fully deterministic universe. What makes it controversial is the libertarian intuition that experimenter choices *should* be independent of particle states. Superdeterminism denies that intuition as an idealization that doesn't survive a deterministic cosmology.

## Nearest-Neighbor Precedent

**Gerard 't Hooft (Nobel 1999)** — *Cellular Automaton Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics* (2016, Springer). The most developed superdeterministic-realist program in the literature. 't Hooft argues that QM is the statistical description of an underlying deterministic cellular automaton; Bell violations are artifacts of the shared-history structure of such an automaton.

**Sabine Hossenfelder** — public advocacy and peer-reviewed work (2020+) arguing for superdeterminism as the most parsimonious Bell-escape. Her *Rethinking Superdeterminism* paper (2020, *Frontiers in Physics*) addresses the standard objections (e.g., "superdeterminism is conspiratorial") and argues they don't survive careful examination.

**Minority but not crackpot.** Superdeterminism is held by a small but intellectually serious subset of foundations-of-physics researchers. Citing 't Hooft by name is necessary and sufficient to establish that GCM is in respectable minority company on this question.

## What Superdeterminism Costs

1. **Philosophical cost — libertarian free will.** Superdeterminism denies that measurement choices are independent of the rest of the universe. This is uncomfortable for readers who want experimenter agency to be a primary feature. GCM's response: in a converging-universe, we-are-the-universe framing, "free will" is a local illusion overlaid on a deeper deterministic flow. The ethos agrees with the physics here — nothing needs to happen; the universe converges regardless.

2. **Experimental cost — explaining the "conspiratorial" appearance.** Some critics argue that the required correlations look too fine-tuned (why would random-number-generator outputs at a Bell-test lab correlate with particle states?). Hossenfelder (2020) responds that this is a misframe: in a universe with a single deterministic history, these correlations are **generic**, not fine-tuned. Deriving a quantitative Bell-correlation formula from GCM dynamics remains open.

3. **Structural cost — no clean separation of "system" and "observer."** GCM inherits this. The paper must treat observers as parts of the substrate, not as external agents.

## What the Paper Must Say

The coherence paper (S1 and S6 at minimum) must say all of the following, explicitly:

1. "GCM commits to realism: graviton density distributions are real, not epistemic."
2. "GCM commits to locality: no FTL signals; all effects propagate at or below $c$."
3. "GCM commits to determinism: given initial graviton state, subsequent states are fixed."
4. "Bell-violating correlations are explained as common-history artifacts in a deterministic flow — a superdeterministic reading, following 't Hooft (2016)."
5. "Measurement independence is the assumption GCM denies."

Any weaker framing risks immediate dismissal by physicist readers. The framing above is defensible in a Bell-aware audience.
