## The Claim

If gravitons propagate effects through the substrate at a universal speed $c$, then the Lorentz transformations of special relativity are natural descriptions of how observers moving through that substrate measure each other's clocks and rulers. Special relativity (SR) is *consistent* with GCM's minimal model; it is not yet *derived* from it.

This is, in structural terms, Wisdom's 21/5/25 note:

> *"Everything is gravity. Einstein relativity is this."*

Light-speed invariance, time dilation, length contraction, and the relativity of simultaneity follow from any substrate with a universal signal speed. GCM's graviton substrate is one such substrate. SR is not an additional postulate; it is what a substrate-with-universal-signal-speed must produce.

## The Argument

### Step 1 — If Gravitons Propagate at $c$, There is a Universal Signal Speed

Take the minimal-model equation applied to the full graviton substrate. A perturbation (a region of altered density) in the substrate cannot propagate faster than the characteristic signal speed of the substrate itself. In standard field-theoretic terms: the graviton field has a retarded propagator with light-cone structure.

Claim: that characteristic speed is $c$ — the same $c$ appearing in special relativity, in Maxwell's equations (as the electromagnetic signal speed), and in general relativity (as the gravitational-wave speed).

**This is not derived from GCM.** It is assumed, consistent with the fact that photons (structures of gravitons per GCM) propagate at $c$, and gravitational disturbances (also structures of gravitons, per T1) must propagate at the same speed by parity of the substrate. OQ-3 names this as an open problem: the value of $c$ should ultimately be expressible as a function of graviton-substrate parameters ($m_g$, graviton density, interaction strength).

### Step 2 — Robertson (1949): Lorentz Transformations Derived from Substrate + Universal $c$

Howard P. Robertson (1949, *Reviews of Modern Physics* 21: 378) showed that if space has a universal signal speed $c$ and the substrate is homogeneous and isotropic, then the coordinate transformations between observers in uniform relative motion must be the Lorentz transformations — up to a remaining one-parameter freedom that experiment then pins down.

Robertson's derivation does not assume SR's postulates. It assumes:
1. A substrate with a well-defined universal signal speed $c$
2. Spatial isotropy and homogeneity on relevant scales
3. The existence of inertial reference frames

From these, the Lorentz transformation structure (including time dilation and length contraction) follows. This is the classical "substrate derivation" of SR.

**GCM inherits this structure.** Assumption (1) is GCM's hypothesis that gravitons propagate effects at $c$. Assumption (2) is the homogeneous-and-isotropic vacuum (GCM's background $\rho_0$). Assumption (3) is standard. Given all three, Robertson's argument produces the Lorentz transformations on GCM's substrate.

### Step 3 — SR is Compatible with, But Not Derived From, the Minimal Model

What the above shows: if GCM's substrate has properties (1), (2), (3), then SR emerges automatically.

What the above does not show: that GCM's minimal-model equation, applied to a gas of $10^{185}$ gravitons, actually produces a substrate with those properties. In particular:
- We have not shown $c$ emerges dynamically from graviton parameters (OQ-3).
- We have not shown that linearized perturbations around the background density propagate with a light-cone structure respecting SR.
- We have not derived the mass-energy relation $E = mc^2$ from GCM dynamics (though it is consistent with the GCM mass formula $m = m_g\xi$ combined with the rest-energy $E = m_g\xi c^2$).

These are all reasonable conjectures that the minimal-model dynamics would support these properties — but conjectures, not derivations.

## What SR Compatibility Gives GCM

Several desirable consequences follow for free, without additional postulates, once SR compatibility is accepted:

1. **Time dilation for moving photons** — a photon (structure of gravitons) moving with velocity close to $c$ experiences time dilation consistent with SR. This is structural, not a separate postulate.
2. **Light-cone causality** — no graviton-mediated effect propagates faster than $c$. Compatible with the superdeterministic stance on Bell (S5.5(c)) since the "common history" is communicated via the substrate at or below $c$.
3. **Mass-energy equivalence** — $E = mc^2$ with $m = m_g\xi$. Consistent; the photon's small rest mass (L1 in S5) remains compatible because the photon's energy is dominated by $pc$, not $mc^2$.
4. **Length contraction for extended objects** — composite particles moving at relativistic speeds experience length contraction along the motion direction. Structural consequence.

## The Historical Analogy

This is the same move that Lorentz and Poincaré made before Einstein: interpret the Lorentz transformations as properties of the ether (their name for the substrate). The move failed experimentally only because their ether had a preferred rest frame — which is ruled out by Michelson-Morley.

GCM's substrate *does not* have a preferred rest frame at the level of individual gravitons. The minimal-model equation is manifestly Galilean-covariant, and any universal signal speed in a substrate of homogeneous-and-isotropic graviton dynamics will produce Lorentz transformations without a preferred frame — this is Robertson's result. Michelson-Morley is compatible with GCM.

Einstein's innovation was to make SR a direct postulate (universal $c$, principle of relativity) without committing to a substrate. GCM, like Lorentz, keeps the substrate and derives SR from it — but avoids the ether's preferred-frame problem by using a different substrate structure.

## Caveats

- **$c$ is assumed, not derived.** The value of the speed of light as a function of graviton parameters is OQ-3 — the deepest open structural question for GCM's compatibility with relativistic physics.
- **GCM as a "neo-Lorentzian" program.** Keeping the substrate while respecting SR is a philosophical minority position in modern physics. Most physicists prefer Einstein's operationalist reading (no substrate). GCM's commitment to a substrate is consonant with its ontological commitments (graviton as primary); it places GCM in a minority tradition but not an empirically-refuted one.
- **General relativity is another story.** S5.5(b) addresses only *special* relativity. GR — with its curved spacetime, stress-energy sourcing, and gravitational wave dynamics — is a much harder recovery problem. It is treated in S8 as the "semiclassical GR limit" structural challenge, not here.
- **Lorentz invariance tests (e.g., modern Kennedy-Thorndike experiments) are at the $10^{-15}$ precision level.** GCM's compatibility claim is structural, not precision-tested. Should future experiments detect tiny Lorentz-violating effects, GCM would need to address them — plausibly via a substrate-anisotropy argument, but that is future work.
