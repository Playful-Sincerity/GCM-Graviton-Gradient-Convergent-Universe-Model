# E7 — Caveats

## What This Proves

- **Algebraic consistency constraint.** For the Bohr ground state to remain invariant under simultaneous shifts $\Delta e$ and $\Delta m_e$, the ratio $\Delta m_e / m_e = -4 \Delta e / e$ must hold.
- **Compatibility bar for emergent-parameter theories.** Any theory in which $e$ and $m_e$ emerge from a deeper substrate (GCM, Verlinde-style emergent constants, preon programs) must satisfy this ratio to avoid drifting hydrogen spectroscopy — unless the theory also provides a compensating mechanism at a higher order.

## What This Does Not Prove

- **Not a prediction of variability.** The empirical constraint on drift of the fundamental constants is many orders of magnitude tighter than $\Delta e \sim 10^{-35}$ C would produce at Bohr precision. We have no evidence that $e$ or $m_e$ vary.
- **Not a prediction for GCM specifically.** E7 is a consistency constraint on *any* emergent-parameter theory. GCM is one such theory; so is Verlinde's emergent gravity; so (in spirit) are preon programs. E7 does not distinguish GCM.
- **Not a QED-level result.** The Bohr formula ignores:
  - Relativistic corrections (Dirac equation replaces $-m_e k^2 e^4 / (2\hbar^2)$ with the Dirac hydrogen spectrum).
  - Radiative corrections (Lamb shift).
  - Hyperfine structure (proton magnetic moment).
  - Nuclear finite-size and reduced-mass corrections.
  At the precision where $\Delta e \sim 10^{-35}$ C would be resolvable, every one of these dominates the naive $E_1$ dependence. The ratio $-4$ is specific to the Bohr level.

## Important Subtlety — Are $e$ and $m_e$ Cleanly Separable?

In full QFT, the "electron charge" that enters scattering amplitudes is not a single number — it runs with energy (the $\beta$-function of QED). The "electron mass" is similarly a running parameter, renormalized at each scale. GCM's reading — that $e$ and $m_e$ are independent parameters to be varied — is valid at the Bohr-QM level, where both are constants. In QFT, the constraint needs to be restated in terms of renormalized couplings at a given scale. The coherence-paper presentation should flag this: E7 is honest at Bohr; a QED extension is future work.

## Framing for the Coherence Paper

E7 belongs in S2 precisely because it is easy to derive, exact at the Bohr level, and illustrative of *the kind of constraint* an emergent-parameter theory must satisfy. It is a "consistency bar," not a "prediction." The language in the paper should reflect that — present as: *"If GCM's graviton-substrate story produces $e$ and $m_e$ as emergent parameters, the Bohr ground state requires them to co-vary with ratio $-4$. This is a compatibility condition GCM must respect in its full treatment."*

## Open Questions Surfaced

- **Extending to higher-$n$ energies.** Does the same $-4$ ratio hold for the excited states of hydrogen? (Yes: all Bohr energies share the $m_e k^2 e^4 / (\hbar n)^2$ form, so the exponent structure is identical. The ratio is level-independent.)
- **Extending to other observables.** Atomic transitions, ionization energies, and binding energies inherit the same ratio at Bohr level. Other observables (magnetic moment, g-factor) depend on additional constants and yield different ratios.
- **Does GCM offer a mechanism by which the ratio is naturally satisfied?** Not yet. This is the frontier — if gravitons generate both $e$ (via flow asymmetry, §H2) and $m_e$ (via graviton count, §H6), the two mechanisms would need to co-adjust coherently. That's a research agenda, not a current claim.

## Relationship to Other Claims

- **Independent of M1, Q3, L2, M3** at the formula level.
- **Structurally related to H2 (charge as flow asymmetry) and H6 (mass from graviton count).** E7 is the constraint any joint H2+H6 derivation must respect.
- **Related in spirit to T1** — "one fundamental particle, all else emergent" is the claim that forces the $e$-and-$m_e$ co-emergence question in the first place.
