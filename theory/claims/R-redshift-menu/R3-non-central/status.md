# R3 — Status

**Epistemic tier:** 🔴 Red

**One-line status:** Structurally elegant (automatic time dilation via GR) but requires a cosmological anisotropy that current observations tightly constrain and GCM has not specified.

## Why Red (not Yellow)

- No quantitative model of the convergence center's location, distance from us, or potential structure.
- The required gravitational anisotropy has not been measured; current isotropy bounds are stringent.
- The claim involves a load-bearing anthropic question (why are we positioned where the gradient produces $H_0$?) with no answer.
- Unlike R1 and R2, which have peer-reviewed cousins (tired-light critiques and Wiltshire timescape respectively), R3 has no peer-reviewed cousin. "We are in a local void" proposals (void cosmology, e.g., Alexander et al.) exist but are different mechanisms.

## What R3 Has Going For It

- Time dilation is automatic via GR — no R6 companion required.
- Blackbody preservation is automatic.
- Tolman scaling plausibly recovered.
- The Secrest et al. 2021 radio-galaxy dipole misalignment with CMB dipole (4.9σ) is an anomaly that *could* be consistent with a gravitational dipole component. This is a thin thread, but it is a thread.

## What Would Promote R3 to Yellow

1. **Specify the convergence center.** Use GCM's convergence dynamics (T3) to produce a predicted direction and distance for the center.
2. **Compute the required potential gradient.** Derive $d\Phi/dr$ from GCM and check that it gives $z \sim H_0 d / c$ at cosmological distances without fine-tuning.
3. **Predict the CMB gravitational dipole.** Separate the kinematic and gravitational contributions to the observed dipole using their different frequency signatures. Compare to data.
4. **Address the Secrest anomaly.** If GCM can produce a quantitative match to the radio-galaxy-vs-CMB dipole offset, R3 jumps to yellow.

## What Would Demote R3 Further

- A tighter isotropy bound that rules out the required gradient magnitude.
- Resolution of the Secrest anomaly via a $\Lambda$CDM mechanism that removes R3's supporting evidence.

## Comparison to Peer-Reviewed Alternatives

- **Void cosmology (Alexander 2007, Wiltshire 2009 "fractal bubble" model, etc.):** proposes we sit in a large local underdensity. Different in structure from R3 (which is about bulk potential gradient, not local underdensity), but shares the "non-central position explains apparent acceleration" spirit.
- **Moffat MOND variants:** gravitational modifications that can produce apparent acceleration without dark energy. Structurally different (modified gravity law) but similar in eliminating $\Lambda$.
- **Secrest-dipole interpretations:** in $\Lambda$CDM this is an unresolved anomaly. R3 could in principle explain it; no quantitative match has been produced.

## Honest Flag

R3 is the weakest of the six mechanisms as currently specified. It is included in the menu for completeness — because the "we are not at the center" reading of T3 is a real GCM possibility — but it is not a serious candidate for primary commitment at this stage. Promotion to yellow requires a specific GCM-derived prediction that can be checked. Without one, R3 is a placeholder, not a candidate.

**If R2's ISW tension becomes unresolvable and R5's time-dilation gap is unresolvable, R3's "automatic time dilation" becomes attractive enough to revisit seriously.** Until then, the menu's primary candidates are R2 and R5, with R6 companion for time dilation.
