# R3 — Empirical Check

Evaluation of R3 (non-central position) against the seven empirical constraints.

## 1. SNe Ia Light-Curve Time Dilation — $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Highest.

Gravitational redshift automatically produces time dilation at the same factor. R3 passes this test "for free" — no R6 companion needed. This is R3's main structural advantage.

**Status:** 🟢 Structurally passes; requires the gravitational-redshift gradient to produce exactly $(1+z)$, which it does by GR's equivalence principle.

## 2. ISW Effect

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

R3 does not require void growth. The cosmological potential is, in R3's picture, a large-scale (bulk) structure that evolves slowly on the timescale of matter convergence. Whether this evolution produces an observable ISW signal depends on the rate of convergence.

Slow-evolution R3 could be ISW-silent (potential quasi-static). Fast-evolution R3 produces an ISW signal that would need to match observed CMB-LSS cross-correlation. Without a quantitative model, neither prediction can be checked.

**Status:** 🟡 Not a direct tension like R2; but also not a resolved empirical prediction.

## 3. BAO — 150 Mpc Acoustic Standard Ruler

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

BAO distances are derived from early-universe physics. R3 doesn't change the sound horizon at recombination. But R3 does change the distance-redshift relation: a source at redshift $z$ via gravitational redshift is at a different proper distance than in $\Lambda$CDM.

Whether R3's distance-redshift relation matches DESI DR2 BAO measurements is an open quantitative question.

**Status:** 🔴 Open. No model to check.

## 4. BBN — Primordial Nucleosynthesis

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

R3 is silent on early-universe physics. BBN is deferred to the broader GCM cosmology.

**Status:** 🔴 Not addressed by R3.

## 5. CMB Blackbody Spectrum

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

Gravitational redshift is purely geometric and preserves blackbody shape. A blackbody at temperature $T_s$ at the source, redshifted gravitationally by factor $(1+z)$, appears as a blackbody at $T_s/(1+z)$ to the observer. This is the same as adiabatic cooling.

**Status:** 🟢 Preserves blackbody shape.

## 6. CMB Anisotropy / Isotropy — The Actual Problem for R3

**This is R3's central empirical difficulty**, distinct from the generic "CMB blackbody" check.

**Observed:** The CMB is isotropic to $10^{-5}$ after dipole subtraction. The dipole (3.36 mK) is currently attributed entirely to our peculiar velocity (~369 km/s).

**R3 predicts:** A gravitational contribution to the dipole (and possibly higher multipoles) from our non-central position. This contribution should appear in the dipole amplitude and frequency dependence.

**Testable:** Kinematic and gravitational dipoles have different frequency signatures. Kinematic is a pure Doppler (blueshift/redshift); gravitational is pure redshift that doesn't change with frequency beyond the standard blackbody temperature shift.

**Current bounds:** Existing CMB analyses are consistent with pure kinematic dipole within observational precision. If there is a gravitational component, it is sub-dominant.

**Suggestive anomaly:** Secrest et al. 2021 found the dipole in quasar and galaxy counts is misaligned with the CMB dipole at 4.9σ — unresolved in $\Lambda$CDM. Whether R3 can account for this is unknown without a quantitative model.

**Status:** 🔴 Tension with observed near-isotropy; suggestive anomaly exists but R3 cannot quantitatively explain it yet.

## 7. Tolman Surface Brightness

**Severity:** ⭐⭐ Medium.

Gravitational redshift produces the full $(1+z)^{-4}$ surface-brightness dimming when combined with standard angular-diameter-distance scaling in a GR framework. R3 inherits this behavior. In a non-expanding GR framework with gravitational potential, Tolman scaling is preserved.

**Status:** 🟢 Plausibly consistent.

## 8. DESI DR2 — Weakening Dark Energy

**Severity:** ⭐ Low-medium.

R3 eliminates dark energy by replacing it with bulk gravitational redshift. DESI DR2's weakening DE is consistent.

**Status:** 🟢 Supportive.

## The Key Failure Mode

R3's structural elegance (automatic time dilation, preserved blackbody, Tolman consistency) is undercut by the isotropy problem. Current observations strongly constrain any cosmological gravitational anisotropy; R3 requires exactly such an anisotropy at a specific magnitude.

The Secrest et al. 2021 CMB-vs-radio-galaxy dipole misalignment is intriguing but does not (yet) pin down a gravitational cause. Until R3 produces a specific predicted anisotropy direction and magnitude that can be searched for, the "where is the convergence center?" question dominates the empirical picture.
