## The Claim

A photon is a traveling graviton-cluster structure. The photon's wavelength reflects the internal spacing between graviton clusters in its structure. When a photon is redshifted by any mechanism (R1–R5), its wavelength increases by the factor $(1+z)$. By GCM's wavelength-to-spacing correspondence (L3), the internal graviton-cluster spacing increases by the same factor.

Increased internal spacing means longer paths for information to propagate within the photon structure. The photon's intrinsic "clock" — the rate at which its internal structure evolves from our observer-frame perspective — ticks more slowly by the same factor.

**The structural cascade:**

$$\lambda_{\text{obs}} = (1+z) \cdot \lambda_{\text{emit}} \quad \text{(by definition of redshift)}$$

$$d_{\text{internal}} \propto \lambda \quad \text{(by L3, wavelength-spacing correspondence)}$$

$$\tau_{\text{internal}} \propto d_{\text{internal}} \quad \text{(internal information delay scales with spacing)}$$

Therefore:

$$\tau_{\text{obs}} = (1+z) \cdot \tau_{\text{emit}}$$

This gives exactly the $(1+z)$ time dilation required by the DES 2024 SNe Ia measurement.

## Why This is Important

The DES 2024 measurement, $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$, is the single sharpest empirical constraint on non-expansion cosmology. Any mechanism producing redshift without also producing $(1+z)$ time dilation is classical tired light and is ruled out at ~200σ.

R6 is the GCM-specific answer to this constraint. It is not a redshift mechanism on its own — it does not explain why wavelengths stretch. It is a *companion*: given that wavelengths have stretched by $(1+z)$ (via whatever primary mechanism R1–R5 provides), R6 explains why the associated temporal structure also stretches by $(1+z)$.

## Why the Cascade Matters

The cascade is structural, not coincidental. If photons were point particles with no internal structure, $(1+z)$ time dilation would be mysterious in any non-expansion framework. The GCM framing — photons as extended graviton-cluster structures — makes time dilation a geometric consequence of wavelength stretching.

This is directly analogous to how timescape cosmology (Wiltshire) escapes the SNe Ia test: timescape's metric includes explicit time dilation (clocks in voids tick ~35% faster than clocks in walls), so $(1+z)$ time dilation is baked in. R6 does the same via photon internal structure rather than via clock-rate differentials at the source/observer locations.

## What R6 Is Not

- **R6 is not a redshift mechanism.** It does not explain why wavelengths stretch. It explains why time also stretches once wavelengths have.
- **R6 is not a claim that photons have internal degrees of freedom the standard model doesn't allow.** GCM's ontology has only one fundamental particle (the graviton); what "photon" means in GCM is already a composite structure by Tenet T1. R6 is just a statement about how the internal structure responds to wavelength changes.
- **R6 is not a proven derivation.** The cascade is a plausibility argument at the structural level. A rigorous derivation would require a specific model of the photon's internal graviton organization.

## Relation to L3 (Wavelength-Spacing Correspondence)

R6 depends on L3: the claim that photon wavelength is proportional to internal graviton-cluster spacing. L3 is itself a yellow-tier claim (dimensionally motivated, not formally derived). If L3 fails — if wavelength is proportional to something else — R6's cascade breaks at the second step.

This dependency is explicit. R6 does not claim more than "if L3 holds, then the cascade gives $(1+z)$ time dilation."

## The Structural Argument in One Paragraph

A photon, in GCM, is an extended graviton-cluster structure with internal spacing $d$ proportional to its wavelength $\lambda$. When the photon redshifts — for any reason — its wavelength stretches by $(1+z)$, so its internal spacing stretches by the same factor. Since the rate at which internal structure can update is limited by the internal propagation time (which scales with spacing), a stretched photon has a stretched internal clock. From our observer-frame perspective, we see the photon's intrinsic temporal structure (its "ticks") stretched by the same $(1+z)$. This is the time dilation measured by DES 2024. It falls out of the photon's internal geometry, not from expansion of the universe.

## Honest Framing

**Green (structural):** The cascade argument is clean and consistent. Given GCM's ontology, it is what the framework predicts for photon time dilation under any redshift mechanism.

**Yellow (formal):** A rigorous derivation would require specifying the photon's internal graviton organization (how many graviton clusters, at what spacing, with what connectivity) and deriving the information-propagation time from first principles. This has not been done.

**Required dependencies:** L3 (wavelength-spacing correspondence) must hold. If L3 is wrong, R6 breaks.

**What would falsify R6:** A measurement showing SNe Ia time dilation deviates from $(1+z)$ at a level inconsistent with the cascade structure. DES 2024 measures $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$, consistent with R6's prediction of exact $(1+z)$.
