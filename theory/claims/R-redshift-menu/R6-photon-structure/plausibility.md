# R6 — Plausibility

## The Freight-Train Intuition (from 2026-04-23 chronicle)

Wisdom's session on 2026-04-23 produced a plain-language frame that belongs here: a photon in GCM is like a freight train. Its wavelength is the distance between cars. If the train is stretched (longer gaps between cars), it takes longer for the full train to pass a fixed point. That "takes longer" is the time dilation.

The fixed point is our detector. The train is the photon. The distance between cars is the internal graviton-cluster spacing. If the train's cars are spread out by factor $(1+z)$, the train takes $(1+z)$ times longer to pass — which is exactly the observed SNe Ia light-curve widening.

This intuition is why the cascade is structural, not ad hoc: any extended structure in space whose internal spacing scales with wavelength will show this behavior.

## The Structural Argument (Rigorous Form)

**Premises:**

1. **GCM ontology (T1):** the photon is a composite structure of gravitons, not a point particle.
2. **L3:** photon wavelength $\lambda$ is proportional to internal graviton-cluster spacing $d_{\text{internal}}$.
3. **Information-propagation bound:** the internal dynamics of the photon cluster (whatever makes the cluster behave coherently as a traveling photon) are limited by information propagation at a characteristic speed $v_{\text{int}}$ across the internal spacing.

**Consequence:**

$$\tau_{\text{internal tick}} \sim \frac{d_{\text{internal}}}{v_{\text{int}}}$$

Under redshift with factor $(1+z)$:

$$\lambda' = (1+z) \lambda \Rightarrow d'_{\text{internal}} = (1+z) d_{\text{internal}} \Rightarrow \tau'_{\text{internal tick}} = (1+z) \tau_{\text{internal tick}}$$

provided $v_{\text{int}}$ is unchanged. This is the $(1+z)$ time dilation.

## Why $v_{\text{int}}$ Should Be Approximately Unchanged

The internal propagation speed $v_{\text{int}}$ in a photon-cluster is a property of graviton-to-graviton interaction dynamics, which are set by the graviton mass and the local substrate density. Assuming the local substrate density at the detector is not wildly different from at the source (a reasonable assumption for most cosmological paths, given that the photon crosses many voids and walls on the way and averages over them), $v_{\text{int}}$ is approximately the same at emission and detection.

This is an assumption, not a derivation. It is consistent with what GCM says about the substrate. It would need to be checked for source/detector pairs in very different density environments.

## Numerical Consistency with DES 2024

**Measured:** $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$ (DES 2024, 1,504 SNe Ia, $z \sim 0.1$ to $1.2$).

**R6 prediction:** exact $(1+z)$, i.e., exponent $= 1$.

**Consistency:** the measurement is consistent with exponent $= 1$ at 1σ — the best-fit value is 1.003 with a statistical uncertainty of ±0.005, so exponent = 1.000 falls within one sigma.

## Comparison to Timescape

Wiltshire's timescape cosmology produces $(1+z)$ time dilation via clock-rate differentials in the metric: clocks in voids tick faster than clocks in walls by ~35%. When we observe distant sources, we are observing their intrinsic source-frame time stretched by our observer-frame clock relative to theirs.

Timescape's mechanism is *GR backreaction on inhomogeneity* — a geometric effect of standard GR applied to a realistic cosmic web. R6's mechanism is *photon internal structure responding to wavelength* — a geometric effect at the photon scale, not the cosmological scale.

These are distinct mechanisms with the same observational consequence. They can be argued independently.

## Where the Argument Needs Strengthening

1. **L3 is itself yellow.** Wavelength-spacing correspondence has a dimensional motivation (a photon's wavelength sets the natural length scale of its internal structure) but no derivation. If the actual photon internal structure has wavelength-independent or wavelength-nonlinear features, the cascade breaks.

2. **Internal information-propagation speed.** Treated as fixed, but in principle could depend on the substrate the photon is currently in. For cosmological photons averaging over voids and walls, fluctuations in $v_{\text{int}}$ could produce scatter in the time-dilation measurement — which is observed at about 0.5% (the $\pm 0.005$ in the DES result). Whether this scatter comes from R6 fluctuations, intrinsic SNe Ia diversity, or measurement noise is not distinguishable currently.

3. **No detailed photon structure model.** How many graviton clusters make up a photon? What is their coordination? What keeps them together as a traveling unit? These are fundamental unresolved questions in the GCM ontology of photons, and R6 assumes a generic answer.

## Falsification Routes

- **Tighter SNe Ia measurements.** If future surveys (LSST, Euclid, Roman) measure the time-dilation exponent to precision 0.001, and the exponent is statistically inconsistent with 1.000, R6 is falsified.
- **Alternative structure argument.** If a specific GCM photon model shows that the time-dilation exponent should differ from 1 by some calculable amount, R6 becomes more specific and testable.
- **Alternative L3 relation.** If L3 turns out to be wavelength-quadratic or wavelength-independent for some range, the cascade shifts.

## Why R6 is Load-Bearing

Every R1–R5 mechanism that survives the menu does so via R6 (except R3, which has GR-automatic time dilation). If R6 fails — if the cascade is structurally wrong — then the entire menu (except R3) collapses against SNe Ia. R6 is the thread holding most of the menu together.

This is acknowledged honestly. R6 is the most important single mechanism in the menu, even though it is not a redshift mechanism itself.
