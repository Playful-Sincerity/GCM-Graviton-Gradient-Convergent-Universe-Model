# R4 — Empirical Check

R4 (light-pressure sensitivity) is not specified precisely enough for detailed empirical check. Where possible, generic constraints are applied.

## 1. SNe Ia Light-Curve Time Dilation

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Highest.

A pressure-frequency coupling is not obviously a time-dilation mechanism. Shifting frequency without stretching time reproduces classical tired light — ruled out at ~200σ. R4 therefore requires R6 companion or it fails outright.

**Status:** 🔴 Fails without R6. With R6: contingent.

## 2. ISW

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

If R4 is a local instantaneous effect (no path memory), it doesn't produce an ISW signal — and doesn't explain the observed one. If R4 is a path-integrated effect, it is structurally R2 and shares R2's ISW tension.

**Status:** 🟡 Ambiguous depending on mechanism specification.

## 3. BAO

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

Without a specific $\nu = f(\rho_g)$ and a distance-redshift relation, R4 cannot be checked against BAO.

**Status:** 🔴 Not addressed.

## 4. BBN

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

R4 is a statement about photon-substrate coupling in the present substrate. Silent on early-universe physics.

**Status:** 🔴 Not addressed.

## 5. CMB Blackbody Spectrum

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

This is R4's most specific vulnerability. A pressure-sensitive coupling likely depends on frequency (different wavelengths probe the substrate at different scales). Frequency-dependent coupling would distort blackbody spectra.

FIRAS precision on the CMB blackbody is ~50 ppm. Any frequency-dependent R4 coupling must be below this level.

**Status:** 🔴 Strong constraint; requires R4 to be frequency-independent at FIRAS precision, which has no a priori justification.

## 6. Tolman Surface Brightness

**Severity:** ⭐⭐ Medium.

Depends on whether R4 is path-integrated or local.

**Status:** 🟡 Ambiguous.

## 7. DESI DR2

**Severity:** ⭐ Low-medium.

R4 eliminates the need for dark energy at the level that any non-expansion mechanism does.

**Status:** 🟢 Supportive.

## The Deeper Problem

R4 cannot be meaningfully empirically checked because it is under-specified. The empirical check above is more about "what constraints would apply if R4 were fleshed out" than about "does R4 fit current data."

Until a specific $\nu = f(\rho_g)$ is proposed — with a clear distinction from R1, R2, R3, R5 — empirical evaluation is speculative.
