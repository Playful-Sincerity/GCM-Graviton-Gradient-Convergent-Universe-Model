# R4 — Plausibility

## The Intuition

In GCM, a photon is a traveling graviton structure. The substrate the photon passes through is itself made of gravitons. Any localized photon-cluster will couple to the ambient graviton density in some way — if only because the "photon" is not a separate substance, it is the same substance as the medium.

The question R4 asks: does this coupling produce a *pressure sensitivity* — does the photon's frequency track the local graviton density (or its derivatives) in a way that cumulatively produces cosmological redshift?

## Why This Is a Weak Mechanism as Specified

R4 suffers from a core specification problem. Pressure in a substrate is a local, present-time quantity. If a photon's frequency is set by the local pressure *now*, then the observed frequency should depend on our location, not on where the photon came from.

Three possible repairs:

**Repair 1: Pressure-memory.** Photons retain a record of the pressures they have passed through, accumulated along the path. Observed frequency reflects the path-integrated pressure.

This makes R4 structurally identical to R2 (void decompression) or R1 (space friction) — a transit-accumulated effect. R4 loses its distinguishing feature.

**Repair 2: Source-side pressure.** Photons carry their emission pressure with them. Observed frequency reflects the pressure at the emission point, not the local one.

This is closer to a "reverse tired light": redshift arises from differences between emission-site pressure and observer-site pressure. Structurally similar to R3 (gravitational potential differences), though with pressure instead of potential.

**Repair 3: Pressure-gradient sensitivity.** Photons couple to pressure *gradients* rather than pressure itself. Traversing regions of decreasing pressure shifts them red; increasing pressure, blue.

This is a specific form of R2: the pressure-gradient is another name for the density-gradient in GCM (since graviton density and pressure are coupled). The void decompression mechanism *is* a form of pressure-gradient sensitivity.

## What a Distinct R4 Would Look Like

For R4 to be a mechanism independent of R1, R2, R3, R5, it would need to claim something like:

- Photon internal structure responds to *instantaneous* local pressure via a reversible coupling.
- This coupling has a specific functional form $\nu = f(\rho_g)$ derived from GCM dynamics.
- The path-averaged effect of this coupling across cosmological distances produces the observed $(1+z)$ redshift.

None of this has been specified. The mechanism as it stands is conceptual — a gesture toward something that might exist in GCM's force-unification picture — rather than a worked out proposal.

## Dimensional Sketch (Placeholder)

If the pressure-frequency relation is $\nu \propto \rho_g^k$ with $k \approx 1/3$ (a pressure-of-an-ideal-gas-like scaling), and if the mean substrate density along a cosmological path is lower than local density by a factor $\rho_{\text{avg}}/\rho_{\text{local}}$, then:

$$\frac{\Delta\nu}{\nu} \approx k \cdot \frac{\Delta\rho_g}{\rho_g}$$

For $\Delta\rho_g/\rho_g \sim 1$ over the Hubble scale, this gives $\Delta\nu/\nu \sim 1/3$ — too large for low-$z$, but a sign that the right coupling could produce the right-order effect.

**This is a placeholder estimate, not a derivation.** The real form of $\nu = f(\rho_g)$ in GCM has not been worked out.

## Where R4 Might Contribute

R4 might be best understood not as an independent redshift mechanism but as an additional effect on top of R2. In R2, wavelength is stretched by void transit. R4 would add a residual frequency-dependent effect from photon-substrate coupling that could, in principle, explain frequency-dependent deviations in the Hubble diagram (if any exist).

Currently no such deviations are observed at significant levels, which neither supports nor falsifies R4.

## Honest Framing

R4 as specified is not a viable primary mechanism. It is retained in the menu as a conceptual placeholder for a family of "photon-substrate coupling" effects that may or may not be real in GCM. Promotion to yellow requires a specific pressure-frequency relation derived from GCM dynamics, with a clear distinction from R2.
