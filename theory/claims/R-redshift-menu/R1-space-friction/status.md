# R1 — Status

**Epistemic tier:** 🟡 Yellow

**One-line status:** Plausible with R6 companion; survives SNe Ia if R6 holds; CMB blackbody is the sharpest specific vulnerability.

## Why Yellow (not Green)

- Friction coefficient $\alpha$ is dimensionally fixable at $H_0/c$ but has no GCM-derived origin; coupling mechanism between photon-cluster and substrate is not specified.
- Requires R6 companion to escape tired-light falsification — R1 does not stand on its own.
- CMB blackbody preservation demands frequency-independent friction to FIRAS precision; no derivation that this is the case.

## Why Not Red

- Structural story is consistent with GCM ontology (photons as graviton structures in a graviton medium).
- Linear redshift-distance relation at low $z$ falls out naturally.
- No direct ISW tension (unlike R2).
- No immediate falsification analogous to tired-light's zero-time-dilation death — R1 + R6 produces $(1+z)$ time dilation.

## What Would Promote R1 to Green

1. **Derive $\alpha$ from GCM first principles** — a specific coupling mechanism between a photon-cluster and the ambient graviton substrate, with $\alpha = f(\rho_g, m_g, \sigma_g)$ yielding $\sim H_0/c$ without fine-tuning.
2. **Demonstrate frequency independence** — show that the friction coefficient does not depend on photon frequency to at least FIRAS precision (50 ppm), or show that frequency dependence exactly mimics adiabatic cooling.
3. **Quantitative fit to Pantheon+ Hubble diagram** — demonstrate R1 + R6 reproduces SNe Ia $\mu(z)$ across $0 < z < 1.5$ at least as well as $\Lambda$CDM.
4. **BAO consistency** — show that the 150 Mpc peak at multiple observed redshifts is consistent with R1's distance-redshift relation.

## What Would Demote R1 to Red

- Evidence that the coupling must be frequency-dependent at a level FIRAS rules out.
- A derivation showing that R6's time-dilation cascade does not produce exactly $(1+z)$ scaling — this would falsify R1 + R6 together.

## Comparison to Peer-Reviewed Alternatives

- **Classical tired light (Zwicky 1929):** ruled out. R1 must not be conflated with this.
- **Wiltshire timescape:** a geometrically different mechanism (inhomogeneous time dilation, not substrate friction). Timescape survives SNe Ia because its metric bakes in time dilation; R1 borrows the same trick via R6.
- **R1's peer-reviewed cousin:** no direct analog. Tired-light variants in the literature generally fail the SNe Ia test precisely because they lack R1's R6-like companion.

## Honest Flag

The "friction coefficient from first principles" problem is R1's load-bearing gap. Without it, R1 is parametrically equivalent to a postulated $H_0$ — which is to say, no improvement over $\Lambda$CDM as a pure fit. R1's claim to non-triviality rests on the GCM-derived nature of $\alpha$, which has not been established.
