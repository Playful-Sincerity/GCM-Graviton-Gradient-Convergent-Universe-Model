## The Claim

Light loses energy to friction with the graviton substrate as it propagates through space. Every photon traversing the graviton medium loses a fraction of its energy per unit distance to the medium itself — not to scattering off specific particles, but to viscous coupling with the substrate as a whole. This energy loss manifests as a decrease in photon frequency (increase in wavelength), producing a redshift that grows with distance.

In GCM's one-particle ontology, this is not a strange claim: photons are themselves graviton structures propagating through a graviton medium. Some degree of coupling between the photon's internal structure and the surrounding substrate is expected. The friction coefficient $\alpha$ parameterizes the strength of this coupling.

**Proposed functional form (dimensional sketch):**

$$\frac{dE}{dr} = -\alpha \cdot E$$

This gives $E(r) = E_0 \cdot e^{-\alpha r}$, and for small $z$: $1 + z \approx e^{\alpha r} \approx 1 + \alpha r$, i.e., $z \propto r$ at low redshift — the Hubble relation.

**What this is not:** R1 is not classical tired light (Zwicky 1929). Classical tired light is definitively falsified because it produces redshift with zero time dilation. R1 requires the R6 companion (photon-structure geometric time dilation) to produce the required $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$ time dilation observed in SNe Ia light curves. R1 alone = tired light; R1 + R6 = a different structural claim.

**Open parameter:** The friction coefficient $\alpha$ has no derivation from first principles yet. Its value must be consistent with cosmological observations across the full redshift range (not just linear $z \ll 1$ regime). See `plausibility.md` for dimensional constraints.
