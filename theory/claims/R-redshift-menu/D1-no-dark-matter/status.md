# D1 — Status

**Epistemic tier:** 🟡 Yellow

**One-line status:** Empirically plausible. Structurally consistent with GCM ontology. Positioned against Profumo PBH-as-DM program (asteroid-mass window 10¹⁷–10²³ g, currently the least-constrained PBH region). Specific halo profiles and CMB power-spectrum fits for D1-consistent models are future work; they are being done in parallel by the PBH community.

## Why Yellow (not Red)

- Every empirical test of dark matter is either neutral or supportive toward D1.
- Asteroid-mass PBH window is open; $f_{\text{PBH}} = 1$ is currently allowed.
- PBH-as-DM is an active, peer-reviewed research program (Profumo group, Carr group, Kuhnel group).
- Decades of WIMP direct-detection null results support the broader "no new particle" direction.
- JWST early-massive-BH anomalies support the broader PBH program.
- Bullet Cluster and gravitational lensing are consistent with collisionless PBH DM.

## Why Not Green

- No specific GCM-derived halo density profile.
- No specific GCM prediction for the PBH mass function (why asteroid-mass, why not other masses).
- CMB power-spectrum fit with PBH DM has been done in the literature but is not GCM-specific work.
- Dwarf-galaxy rotation-curve diversity (cusp-core problem) is not specifically addressed by D1.
- D1 doesn't make predictions distinguishable from non-GCM PBH-DM models at current observational precision.

## What Would Promote D1 to Green

1. **Derive the halo profile.** Show from GCM dynamics what the invisible-matter distribution around a galaxy should be. Compare to NFW/Einasto fits.
2. **Derive the PBH mass function from GCM.** Explain *why* invisible ordinary matter would be predominantly in the asteroid-mass window rather than spread across the full range. (This is a hard problem; the PBH community is actively working on primordial power spectra that could produce this.)
3. **Predict a GCM-specific observational signature.** Find something D1 predicts that vanilla PBH-as-DM does not. This would differentiate D1 from being a mere alignment with the PBH program.
4. **Quantitative CMB-power-spectrum fit.** Compute the D1-specific CMB power spectrum and compare to Planck; show it matches at the level $\Lambda$CDM matches.

## What Would Demote D1 to Red

- Closure of the asteroid-mass PBH window by new observations (femtolensing from high-cadence surveys, CMB spectral distortions, 21cm absorption features). This would force D1 to retreat to non-PBH invisible-matter candidates which are less empirically developed.
- A direct detection of a dark matter particle. This would make D1 a redundant framework (the "new particle" is detected).
- A demonstration that CMB or LSS data require dark matter to behave distinctly from any invisible ordinary matter candidate.

## Comparison to Peer-Reviewed Alternatives

**Profumo PBH-as-DM (primary alignment):** D1 is a structural generalization. Profumo's program is quantitatively specific (mass functions, detection strategies, observational constraints); D1 adds the structural story for why invisible ordinary matter is expected in GCM's ontology. *These are complementary programs, not competing.*

**Carr / Kuhnel PBH program:** similar to Profumo's, different emphasis. Active research community. D1 can draw on their literature for observational constraints.

**MOND / Verlinde entropic gravity:** different approach (modified gravity rather than invisible matter). Verlinde eliminates the DM particle by modifying the gravity law. D1 eliminates it by pointing to existing ordinary matter we haven't seen. Both are "no new particle" claims but mechanistically distinct.

**Fuzzy DM / ultralight-axion DM:** still posits a new particle, just one with different properties. D1 is more radical.

**Neutrino dark matter (warm DM):** posits SM neutrinos as some fraction of DM. Mostly ruled out for the bulk of DM (neutrinos are too light to produce structure correctly). D1 is not neutrino DM.

## Strategic Positioning for Outreach

**Profumo outreach alignment:** D1's structural claim + Profumo's quantitative PBH program = a credible paper with a real peer-reviewed collaboration target. The outreach note to Profumo should lead with: "GCM predicts a broad class of invisible ordinary matter; your PBH program is a specific realization of that class; we think the asteroid-mass window is exactly where GCM's logic would place the dominant invisible-matter contribution; can we discuss?"

This is honest, strategic, and non-inflationary. It does not claim GCM solves dark matter; it claims GCM is consistent with — and structurally predicts — the kind of work Profumo is already doing.

## Honest Flag

D1 is the coherence-case version of the claim. It does not produce new quantitative predictions beyond what the PBH community has already produced. GCM's contribution is the *ontological story* — why invisible ordinary matter is expected, why asteroid-mass PBHs make sense in this picture, why the "no new particle" direction is theoretically natural rather than ad hoc.

**Recommendation for the coherence paper:** D1 gets its subsection in S6. The Profumo PBH citation is load-bearing. The structural connection between D1 and R5 (both are "observed distribution reflects selection effects on what reaches us") is worth making explicit. Do not claim D1 solves dark matter. Do claim D1 is the right direction given the empirical state of the field in 2026.
