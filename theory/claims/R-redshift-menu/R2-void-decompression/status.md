# R2 — Status

**Epistemic tier:** 🟡 Yellow

**One-line status:** Strongest peer-reviewed analog (Wiltshire timescape). ISW tension acknowledged, not hand-waved. Central candidate among the menu.

## Why Yellow (not Red)

- Wiltshire's Pantheon+ result (Seifert et al. 2025, ln B > 5) demonstrates that void/wall structure + inhomogeneous time-rate does fit SNe Ia data better than $\Lambda$CDM. The empirical ceiling for void-based mechanisms is high.
- R2 has a clean GCM mechanism: matter convergence → void decompression → photon-wavelength stretching. Not a free parameter.
- ISW tension is interpretive, not a direct falsifier — R2 reinterprets the signal, it does not deny it.
- R2 + R6 passes SNe Ia time dilation structurally; CMB blackbody is plausibly preserved.

## Why Not Green

- No quantitative $\mu(z)$ prediction for R2 that can be compared to Pantheon+.
- No quantitative ISW shape prediction for R2.
- Void-decompression rate is not derived from GCM's minimal-model dynamics — it is inferred to be $\sim H_0$ by matching the observed Hubble relation.
- BAO and BBN treatment is deferred to the broader GCM cosmology, not addressed by R2 itself.

## What Would Promote R2 to Green

1. **Quantitative $\mu(z)$ fit.** Derive R2's distance-modulus predictions and fit Pantheon+ (1,535 SNe Ia). Compare Bayesian evidence against $\Lambda$CDM and against timescape. Target: ln B > 1 against $\Lambda$CDM at minimum.
2. **Quantitative ISW prediction.** Compute the R2 ISW signal for realistic void statistics. Compare to observed CMB-LSS cross-correlation (Planck ISW-Lensing, PT1, and eBOSS galaxy maps).
3. **BAO distance-redshift relation.** Show that R2's distance-redshift relation reproduces the 150 Mpc peak at multiple DESI DR2 redshift slices.
4. **Derive void-decompression rate.** Use the minimal-model N-body simulation (or an analytical treatment of graviton convergence) to predict the void-decompression rate and check that it is $\sim H_0$ without fine-tuning.

## What Would Demote R2 to Red

- A quantitative R2 ISW prediction that clearly does not match observed data.
- A Pantheon+ fit where R2 produces a Hubble diagram substantially worse than $\Lambda$CDM or timescape.
- A demonstration that void-decompression rates in GCM are orders of magnitude below $H_0$ (making R2 quantitatively insufficient to explain the observed Hubble relation).

## Comparison to Peer-Reviewed Alternatives

- **Wiltshire timescape (Seifert et al. 2025, MNRAS Letters 537, L55):** R2's closest ally. Same phenomenology (voids, walls, clock-rate differential); R2 adds graviton-density mechanism and force unification. *R2's positioning paragraph in S9 should lead with timescape as complementary, not competitive.*
- **Verlinde entropic gravity:** different mechanism (entanglement thermodynamics on holographic screens), but shares the "no DM particle" claim. R2 is not a direct alternative to Verlinde's framework; they address different phenomena.
- **Conformal cosmology (varying c with scale factor):** different mechanism, does not address ISW tension, no peer-reviewed empirical fit comparable to Wiltshire.

## Honest Flags

1. **ISW is the central known gap.** R2's story on ISW is a reinterpretation of an observed signal, not a quantitative prediction. Anyone evaluating R2 in detail will land on ISW first. The honest response: "We reinterpret; quantitative prediction is future work. See [reference model]."
2. **No quantitative fit exists yet.** R2 cannot be compared against Pantheon+ in the way timescape already has been. Saying "R2 is structurally analogous to timescape" is the defensible claim; saying "R2 fits data as well as timescape" would require computation not done.
3. **R2 inherits GCM's early-universe gap.** BAO and BBN both depend on early-universe physics that GCM has not specified. R2 is silent here; the broader GCM cosmology owes the answer.
