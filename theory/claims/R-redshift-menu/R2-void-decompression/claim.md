## The Claim

As matter gravitationally collapses into filaments, walls, and clusters, the graviton density in the intervening voids decreases. Voids do not stay in place and watch galaxies form around them — they *decompress*. Light traveling through decompressing voids undergoes wavelength stretching because the local graviton-density-gradient structure — which sets the effective metric in GCM — is relaxing as the photon transits.

In a converging universe, this is not the same as Big Bang expansion. Matter is flowing *inward* toward local attractors; voids are what the matter leaves behind. The overall universe is not expanding. The light-stretching is local to void transits, and most cosmological photons from distant sources pass through many voids on their way to us — so redshift accumulates with distance.

**Proposed structural form (not a derivation):**

$$\frac{d\lambda}{\lambda} \approx \int_{\text{path}} \frac{\partial \rho_g^{-1}}{\partial t} \, d\ell$$

with the observed Hubble relation emerging as the line-of-sight average of void-decompression rates across cosmological paths.

## Why This Fits GCM

In GCM, space is not a coordinate but a relational structure: space is the set of distances between gravitons, and time is the process of those distances changing (T4). As gravitons flow out of voids into matter concentrations, inter-graviton distances in voids grow. A photon propagating through a void is a graviton structure whose internal spacing (its wavelength) reflects the local inter-graviton scale. Local inter-graviton spacing growth → photon wavelength growth → redshift.

## Relation to Wiltshire Timescape

Wiltshire's timescape cosmology posits that clocks in voids tick ~35% faster than clocks in galaxy walls (backreaction from inhomogeneous GR). Seifert et al. 2025 found Bayesian evidence ln B > 5 in favor of timescape over $\Lambda$CDM on the full Pantheon+ sample of 1,535 SNe Ia — a very strong result.

**GCM's positioning move:** timescape's clock-rate differential is a *phenomenological* consequence of GR backreaction on an inhomogeneous universe. GCM's R2 provides a *mechanism* for why the clock-rate structure arises: voids are regions of low graviton density; low graviton density produces the observed time-rate (and light-stretching) differential. Timescape + R2 = complementary programs, not competitors.

## The ISW Tension (Named Up Front)

Growing voids produce evolving gravitational potentials. Photons crossing a growing void pick up a net blueshift on entry and a smaller net redshift on exit — or vice versa — producing a temperature anisotropy that cross-correlates with large-scale structure. This is the Integrated Sachs-Wolfe (ISW) effect, and it is observed at high significance.

In standard cosmology, the observed ISW signal is attributed to dark energy (which causes potentials to decay). In R2, the same signal exists but is attributed to graviton-density-gradient evolution as matter converges. **This is a reinterpretation of an observed signal, not an elimination of it.** The tension is not "R2 produces a signal that isn't observed" — the signal is observed. The tension is "R2 claims the signal is not dark energy, which places the burden on R2 to produce a mechanism that fits the same data."

## R6 Companion

Void decompression produces wavelength stretching but does not automatically produce SNe Ia time dilation $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$. R6 (photon-structure geometric time dilation) is the companion mechanism: wavelength scaling $\propto (1+z)$ → internal graviton-cluster spacing $\propto (1+z)$ → time dilation $\propto (1+z)$. Timescape escapes the SNe Ia test the same way — its metric includes explicit time dilation. R2 + R6 escapes it via the photon's internal structure.
