Evaluation of R2 (void decompression) against the seven empirical constraints. R6 companion assumed where time dilation is required.

## 2. ISW Effect and Void Growth — THE Central Tension

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

This is R2's load-bearing empirical challenge. Growing voids produce evolving gravitational potentials, producing the observed ISW cross-correlation between CMB and large-scale structure.

**R2's position (stated carefully):**

1. The observed ISW signal is real.
2. R2 does not deny that voids are growing and producing this signal.
3. R2 denies that the signal is evidence for dark energy acceleration. The signal is evidence for gravitational potential evolution in a converging universe.
4. In R2, potential evolution is driven by matter convergence into walls (which deepens wall potentials) and void decompression (which shallows void potentials). Photons crossing this evolving structure pick up the observed ISW signal from a non-$\Lambda$ source.
5. Whether the *shape* of the predicted R2 ISW signal matches the observed one is a quantitative question that has not been answered. This is a known open problem.

**What R2 is NOT saying:**
- R2 is not saying "ISW isn't observed."
- R2 is not saying "ISW is small and can be ignored."
- R2 is not saying "ISW is a coincidence."

**What R2 IS saying:**
- The mechanism producing ISW is graviton-density-gradient evolution, not $\Lambda$-driven potential decay.
- This is a reinterpretation with the same observational consequences at first order; the differences appear in the detailed shape and scale-dependence of the signal.
- Testing R2 against ISW data requires a quantitative model. That model does not yet exist.

**Status:** 🟡 Tension acknowledged, partial interpretive resolution, quantitative resolution is future work. Honest flag: this is the single biggest empirical question for R2.

## 3. BAO — 150 Mpc Acoustic Standard Ruler

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

The BAO scale is set by early-universe sound-horizon physics at recombination. R2 does not change this early-universe scale — whatever GCM says about the early universe must produce the same 150 Mpc sound horizon.

The question R2 must answer: does the distance to which this ruler corresponds, at a given observed redshift, match DESI DR2 measurements? In $\Lambda$CDM, the relation $r_{\text{BAO}}(z)$ is derived from the expansion history. In R2, the relation depends on the void-decompression history, which has not been computed.

**Status:** 🔴 Open. Requires a quantitative R2 distance-redshift relation fitted to DESI DR2.

## 4. BBN — Primordial Nucleosynthesis

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

**Status:** 🔴 Not addressed by R2. Open in the larger GCM cosmology.

## 5. CMB Blackbody Spectrum

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

Wavelength stretching in a void transit is frequency-independent if the mechanism is purely geometric (graviton spacing rescales the photon structure uniformly). A blackbody at temperature $T$ transformed by a uniform wavelength stretch $\lambda \to (1+z)\lambda$ becomes a blackbody at $T/(1+z)$ — this is the same shape transformation as adiabatic cooling in expansion.

**Potential risk:** If the void-decompression rate varies along the line of sight, photons of different frequencies might transit voids of different sizes, producing frequency-dependent distortion. This depends on the detailed void statistics.

**Status:** 🟢/🟡 Consistent at first order; at FIRAS precision (50 ppm), needs a quantitative argument that path-dependent effects average out.

## 6. Tolman Surface Brightness

**Severity:** ⭐⭐ Medium.

Standard $(1+z)^{-4}$ scaling decomposes as $(1+z)^{-1}$ from photon energy, $(1+z)^{-1}$ from redshift of photon rate, and $(1+z)^{-2}$ from angular diameter distance. R2 + R6 reproduces the first two via wavelength stretching and time dilation. The $(1+z)^{-2}$ from angular diameter distance is a statement about geometric dilution, which depends on how R2's non-expanding geometry handles solid-angle-subtended at distance.

This is non-trivial. In a static GCM substrate with void structure, the relation between source distance and apparent angular size is not the same as in $\Lambda$CDM. Whether it produces $(1+z)^{-2}$ or something else is an open geometric question.

**Status:** 🟡 Plausible to match with enough geometric structure; not derived.
