# D2 — Plausibility

## The Context: Dark Energy is Under Strain in 2026

Standard $\Lambda$CDM dark energy is the simple cosmological constant: $w = -1$, constant in redshift. This fit the SN Ia data from the late 1990s (Perlmutter 1998, Riess 1998) and has been the orthodoxy since. But:

- **DESI DR2 (2025):** evolving $w(z)$ at 2.8–4.2σ. The simple $\Lambda$ interpretation is inconsistent with the data at several sigma.
- **Seifert et al. 2025 (MNRAS Letters 537, L55):** timescape (no $\Lambda$) fits Pantheon+ at ln B > 5 over $\Lambda$CDM.
- **Cosmological tensions:** $H_0$ tension (local vs. CMB $H_0$ measurements disagree at 5σ), $\sigma_8$ tension (structure amplitude from CMB vs. from late-time LSS measurements), all unresolved in $\Lambda$CDM.

Dark energy isn't falsified, but it's not healthy either. The question "what is dark energy really?" is more open in 2026 than it has been in two decades.

D2 enters this context with a specific claim: it is not a thing. It is a misinterpretation of cosmological redshift. The empirical atmosphere is favorable to this claim.

## Why DESI DR2 is Supportive (Not Proof)

**The observation:** DESI DR2 BAO measurements combined with SNe Ia and CMB data prefer a $w(z)$ that varies with redshift, with $w$ crossing $-1$ around $z \sim 0.5$. Statistical significance: 2.8–4.2σ depending on analysis choices.

**$\Lambda$CDM reading:** this is evidence for *dynamical* dark energy (quintessence or similar), not for the simple cosmological constant.

**D2 reading:** if "dark energy" is an artifact of redshift mis-interpretation, it should appear to evolve with redshift as our measurements become more precise — because the underlying physics is not $\Lambda$ at all. DESI DR2's apparent $w(z)$ evolution is consistent with this: as data improves, the artifact reveals itself to be not-a-constant, and eventually should reveal itself to be not-a-thing.

**The caveat:** 2.8–4.2σ is suggestive but not conclusive. Higher-precision measurements (DESI DR3, Euclid, Roman) over the next 2–5 years will either (a) firm up the $w(z)$ evolution to >5σ, in which case the $\Lambda$-is-fine camp is strained further, or (b) pull back toward $w = -1$, in which case DESI DR2 was a statistical fluctuation. D2's position in the literature is stronger if (a), weaker if (b). Either way, D2 does not *depend* on DESI DR2 — D2 is the claim that there is no dark energy regardless.
