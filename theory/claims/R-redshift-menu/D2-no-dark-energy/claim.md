## The Claim

There is no dark energy — no cosmological constant $\Lambda$, no quintessence field, no accelerating-expansion component in the cosmic energy budget. The observations commonly interpreted as dark-energy acceleration (SNe Ia dimming beyond standard-matter predictions, BAO scale evolution, CMB peak structure) are misinterpretations of the cosmological redshift mechanism.

The R1–R5 menu provides candidate mechanisms for cosmological redshift without expansion. Once redshift is correctly attributed to graviton-density-gradient evolution, void decompression, selection effects, or structural photon physics, the apparent need for "something accelerating the expansion" disappears. The universe is converging (T3), not expanding, and certainly not accelerating.

## The Structural Argument

**Standard cosmology's inference:**
1. SNe Ia at high-$z$ appear dimmer than expected for a decelerating universe.
2. "Dimmer than expected" is interpreted as "further away than standard matter-only cosmology predicts."
3. "Further away at a given $z$" is interpreted as "expansion has accelerated."
4. "Acceleration" is attributed to dark energy ($\Lambda$ or equivalent).

**D2's reply:**
1. Yes, SNe Ia at high-$z$ appear dimmer.
2. "Dimmer" is a joint consequence of redshift + time-dilation + geometric-dilution. In a non-expansion framework, these combine differently.
3. If the redshift mechanism is void decompression (R2) or selection effect (R5) — both yellow-tier — then the distance-modulus curve fits SNe Ia data without requiring acceleration.
4. No acceleration, no dark energy needed.

The mathematical check: whether R2 (or R5) + R6 produces a $\mu(z)$ curve consistent with Pantheon+ data. This has not been explicitly done for GCM, but Wiltshire's timescape does it for its closely-analogous mechanism and wins over $\Lambda$CDM at ln B > 5.

This phenomenology is closely analogous to what GCM's R2 would produce in its converging-universe framework. D2 positions itself alongside timescape: GCM's force-unification substrate provides the *mechanism* for timescape's phenomenology.

**GCM is not competing with timescape on dark energy.** They are allies. GCM + timescape = timescape's empirical fit + GCM's force-unification theory base.

## The DESI DR2 Support

DESI DR2 (2025) provides the first tentative evidence that $w(z) \neq -1$ at the 2.8–4.2σ level. In other words, the cosmological-constant interpretation of dark energy is being undermined by its own precision measurements. Even in $\Lambda$CDM orthodoxy, dark energy is losing the simple "$\Lambda$-is-fine" fit.

D2 reads this as supportive: if dark energy is an artifact of mis-interpreted redshift, it should appear to evolve with redshift as our measurements become more precise — because it is not a real energy component with a fixed equation of state. DESI DR2 shows exactly this pattern at early significance.

**Honest flag:** 2.8–4.2σ is suggestive, not definitive. It is not "proof of no dark energy." It is evidence that $\Lambda$CDM's simple interpretation is under strain. D2 should lean on this as support, not overclaim it as vindication.

## What D2 Inherits from R2's ISW Problem

If D2 leans on R2 as its primary redshift mechanism, D2 inherits R2's ISW tension. The observed ISW signal in $\Lambda$CDM is evidence for dark-energy-driven potential decay. In R2, the same signal is reinterpreted as graviton-density-gradient evolution. D2's "no dark energy" claim depends on this reinterpretation being quantitatively correct — which has not been verified.

**This is the central honest caveat for D2.** The ISW signal is observed. D2 must explain it without dark energy. That explanation rides on R2's quantitative success, which is future work.

If D2 leans on R5 (selection effect) as primary mechanism instead of R2, the ISW problem becomes a different kind: R5 doesn't produce an ISW signal (it's not a potential-evolution mechanism), but it also doesn't explain the observed ISW signal. D2 + R5 leaves the ISW observation needing an independent explanation.
