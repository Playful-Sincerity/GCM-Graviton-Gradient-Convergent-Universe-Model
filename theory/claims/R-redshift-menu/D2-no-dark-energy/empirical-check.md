# D2 — Empirical Check

Evaluation of D2 (no dark energy) against the dark-energy-related empirical constraints. Since D2 rides on R2 (+ R6) or R5 (+ R6) for its quantitative case, the evaluation partly reduces to R2's or R5's empirical check — but D2 has some distinctive tests of its own.

## 1. Type Ia Supernova Hubble Diagram

**Standard cosmology interpretation:** SNe Ia at high $z$ appear dimmer than matter-only expansion predicts → universe is accelerating → dark energy.

**D2's interpretation:** SNe Ia apparent dimming is explained by a non-$\Lambda$ combination of cosmological redshift (R2 or R5), time dilation (R6), and geometric dilution.

**Empirical benchmark:** timescape (Seifert et al. 2025) demonstrates ln B > 5 over $\Lambda$CDM on 1,535 Pantheon+ SNe. This is "very strong" Bayesian evidence. GCM's R2 + R6 aspires to similar but has not been computed.

**Status:** 🟢/🟡 Structurally consistent via timescape alliance; GCM-specific fit is future work.

## 2. DESI DR2 Evolving $w(z)$

**Observation:** $w(z)$ varies with redshift at 2.8–4.2σ, crosses $w = -1$ around $z \sim 0.5$.

**D2's interpretation:** this is consistent with "no dark energy" — the $\Lambda$CDM artifact should appear to evolve as measurements improve. Evolving $w(z)$ is what D2 would predict if pressed for a $\Lambda$CDM-framework description of the non-existent dark energy.

**Status:** 🟢 Supportive at 2.8–4.2σ significance. Not proof.

## 3. ISW Effect

**Observation:** positive CMB-LSS cross-correlation at several sigma. In $\Lambda$CDM, this is evidence for dark-energy-driven potential decay.

**D2's interpretation (via R2):** the observed ISW signal is graviton-density-gradient evolution as matter converges, not $\Lambda$-driven decay. A reinterpretation, not an elimination.

**D2's interpretation (via R5):** R5 does not produce ISW. The observed signal requires an independent explanation.

**Status:** 🟡 via R2 (inherits R2's ISW tension — quantitative shape prediction is future work); 🔴 via R5 (leaves observed signal unexplained).

## 4. BAO Standard Ruler

**Observation:** 150 Mpc acoustic peak in galaxy correlations across multiple redshift bins. In $\Lambda$CDM, this is a consistency check on expansion history (including $\Lambda$).

**D2's interpretation:** distance-redshift relation is different in a non-expanding GCM cosmology. The 150 Mpc ruler must appear at the correct inferred distance at each observed $z$.

**Status:** 🔴 Open. No GCM-specific distance-redshift relation has been computed.

## 5. CMB Power Spectrum

**Observation:** acoustic peaks at $\ell \sim 200, 500, 800$ whose amplitudes depend on $\Omega_\Lambda, \Omega_m, \Omega_b$ at recombination.

**D2's interpretation:** in a converging, non-expanding universe, the CMB formation physics would need to be re-derived. The observed peak structure is a constraint on any alternative cosmology.

**Status:** 🔴 Open. GCM's early-universe physics is not specified.

## 6. Hubble Tension

**Observation:** Local $H_0$ measurements (SH0ES, ~73 km/s/Mpc) disagree with CMB-inferred $H_0$ (Planck, ~67 km/s/Mpc) at ~5σ.

**D2's interpretation:** if "$H_0$" is not really the expansion rate but a fit parameter reflecting the redshift-distance relation, different observational methods can yield different values for structural reasons (different aspects of the relation are probed). A framework where $H_0$ isn't a fundamental expansion rate can accommodate tension naturally.

**Status:** 🟡 Plausibly consistent — D2 can "absorb" the Hubble tension by denying $H_0$ is a fundamental expansion rate. Quantitative story is not developed.

## 7. $\sigma_8$ Tension

**Observation:** Structure amplitude from CMB is slightly larger than from late-time LSS measurements.

**D2's interpretation:** structure formation in a converging (not expanding) universe would have a different growth history than in $\Lambda$CDM. The $\sigma_8$ tension could reflect this.

**Status:** 🟡 Plausibly consistent; quantitative story is not developed.

## 8. Void Counts and Structure Statistics

**Observation:** large-scale void abundance and statistics in DESI, BOSS, and similar surveys. In $\Lambda$CDM, void growth is driven by dark-energy repulsion.

**D2's interpretation:** void growth is driven by matter convergence into walls (R2). Predicts a void-size distribution consistent with converging-universe dynamics.

**Status:** 🟡 Consistent in direction; specific predictions not computed.

## The Central Honest Finding

D2's empirical case rests on:

1. **Timescape's Pantheon+ result (Seifert 2025 ln B > 5):** strong.
2. **DESI DR2 evolving $w(z)$ (2.8–4.2σ):** suggestive.
3. **Multiple tensions in $\Lambda$CDM ($H_0$, $\sigma_8$):** supportive of "$\Lambda$CDM is incomplete" but not directly of D2.

Against:

1. **BAO distance-redshift relation:** no GCM-specific calculation.
2. **CMB power spectrum:** no GCM early-universe physics.
3. **ISW via R5:** unexplained observed signal.

**The load-bearing strategic move:** D2 allies with timescape as the peer-reviewed program that has already done the quantitative work for non-$\Lambda$ cosmology. GCM is not re-deriving what timescape has derived; GCM is providing the force-unification substrate that timescape's phenomenology requires.

This is honest positioning: GCM is not claiming to quantitatively solve dark energy; GCM is claiming to be theoretically consistent with the most empirically successful non-$\Lambda$ alternative.
