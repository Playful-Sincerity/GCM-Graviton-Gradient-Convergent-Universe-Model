# R5 — Status

**Epistemic tier:** 🟡 Yellow

**One-line status:** Structurally distinct from tired light (no energy-loss-in-transit claim); ISW-clean; time-dilation gap requires R6 or a separate companion; no specific microscopic mechanism yet. Wisdom's original intuition; deserves development.

## Why Yellow (not Red)

- Epistemic move is valid and genuinely different from R1–R4. Observed low-energy bias *could* be selection, not transit physics.
- Cleanly avoids tired-light's falsification mode — there is no claim about individual photon energy loss.
- ISW-clean: no void growth required; no conflict with observed ISW signal (doesn't produce it, doesn't conflict with it).
- Blackbody-compatible in the geometric-bias version.
- Consistent with GCM's D1 reasoning (both encode "what we observe is biased by which photons reach us").

## Why Not Green

- No specific microscopic mechanism (gravitational lensing bias, absorption-scattering bias, geometric aperture bias are candidates, none developed).
- Cannot stretch time → cannot explain SNe Ia time dilation on its own. Requires R6 companion.
- Hard to empirically distinguish from R2 at current observational precision without specific predictions.

## What Would Promote R5 to Green

1. **Specify a microscopic mechanism.** Propose a specific filter function $f(d, E)$ for which photons reach us from a source at distance $d$ with energy $E$. Derive it from GCM dynamics.
2. **Derive $z(d)$ from $f(d, E)$.** Show the observed Hubble relation emerges naturally at the right scale.
3. **Address CMB blackbody preservation.** Demonstrate frequency-independence of the filter to FIRAS precision.
4. **Produce an empirical distinction from R2.** Find a predicted observational signature R5 would produce but R2 would not (e.g., an angular-correlation in Hubble-diagram residuals from intervening matter; a specific chromatic signature in deep-field photometry).

## What Would Demote R5 to Red

- Demonstration that any selection-based mechanism producing the observed Hubble relation must be frequency-dependent at a level FIRAS rules out.
- An observational result that directly constrains the emission-to-detection filter function to be inconsistent with Hubble-scale energy bias.

## Comparison to Peer-Reviewed Alternatives

- **No direct peer-reviewed analog.** Most cosmological redshift mechanisms in the literature are in-transit mechanisms. R5's epistemic move — observational selection on which photons reach the observer — is closer to observational statistics (e.g., Malmquist bias on sources) than to physical cosmology.
- **Partial resonance:** photon-axion oscillation models produce selection-like effects (some photons get converted to axions in transit, biasing the observed photon distribution). These are in-transit mechanisms but share R5's "the observed distribution isn't the emitted distribution" structure.
- **Weak lensing magnification bias:** a well-studied selection effect in surveys. R5 is not this — magnification bias operates on source counts, not on redshift inference — but shares the family of "observation biases on cosmological inference."

## Strategic Positioning

Wisdom's intuition ("light doesn't reach us because we're small") encodes a real epistemic move. R5 deserves development into a defined mechanism, and if developed cleanly it may prove the most honest mechanism in the menu:

- No claim about photon physics requiring new particle properties.
- No claim about void growth requiring ISW reinterpretation.
- No claim about convergence centers requiring undetected anisotropies.
- Just: what we observe is biased by what reaches us.

The honest development path: identify the specific GCM mechanism that biases emission-to-detection (gravitational-lensing bias from PBHs, absorption by cold matter in voids/walls, etc.), derive the resulting $f(d, E)$, and check against data.

## Relationship to Other Menu Items

R5 + R6 is a coherent menu pairing, parallel to R2 + R6. The difference:
- R2 + R6: in-transit wavelength stretching + photon-internal time dilation. Structural physics story, matches Wiltshire phenomenology.
- R5 + R6: selection bias on observed photons + photon-internal time dilation. Epistemic story, no peer-reviewed analog but cleaner on ISW.

These are not mutually exclusive. An amalgamation might include both effects — which is exactly the menu approach's point. Wisdom's 2026-04-23 instruction: *"it's probably an amalgamation of a number of different systems."*

## Honest Flag

R5 is included in the menu because it reflects Wisdom's real intuition and has structural merit. It is yellow, not red, because the epistemic move is valid and worth developing. But until a specific mechanism is worked out, R5 is a *type* of candidate, not a defined mechanism.

**Recommendation for the coherence paper:** dedicate a subsection to R5 as "the selection-effect framing," note that it is currently less developed than R2, and flag it as the alternative if R2's ISW tension proves unresolvable. Keep Wisdom's original "we're small" language in the paper — it is a vivid framing and the paper's voice should preserve that.
