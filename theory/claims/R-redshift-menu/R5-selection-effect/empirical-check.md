# R5 — Empirical Check

Evaluation of R5 (selection effect) against the seven empirical constraints. R6 companion assumed for time-dilation-related tests.

## 1. SNe Ia Light-Curve Time Dilation — $(1+z)^{1.003 \pm 0.005}$

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Highest.

**R5 alone:** FAILS. Selection effects on energy do not stretch time. A filtered subset of SNe Ia photons retains the source's intrinsic temporal structure; the observed light curve has the same duration as the emitted one. $(1+z)^{1.003}$ cannot be produced by selection.

**R5 + R6:** R6 provides the time-dilation via photon-structure geometric stretching. This pairing is logically consistent — R5 explains the energy/frequency bias of what reaches us, R6 explains why the light curve takes longer to unfold. Two different aspects of the observed "redshift."

**R5 + (timescape-like mechanism):** If independent clock-rate differentials exist between source region and observer region (as in Wiltshire), the time dilation comes from clock-rate structure and R5 only handles the energy side.

**Status:** 🔴 Alone; 🟢 with R6.

## 2. ISW Effect

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

R5 does not require void growth or evolving potentials. The selection mechanism operates on which photons reach us, not on how potentials evolve. R5 is compatible with quasi-static gravitational potentials and therefore does not produce an ISW signal.

The observed ISW signal still needs an explanation, but R5 doesn't add one either. Silent, not-tension.

**Status:** 🟢 No direct tension. Observed ISW signal unexplained by R5 but also not required.

## 3. BAO — 150 Mpc Acoustic Standard Ruler

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High.

In R5, distances are inferred via Hubble-diagram-style arguments. If the selection fraction $f(d)$ and energy-bias $\langle E \rangle_{\text{received}}/\langle E \rangle_{\text{emitted}}$ both depend systematically on distance, the inferred distance from a given $z$ differs from the standard cosmology's inferred distance.

Whether the BAO 150 Mpc ruler appears at the correct inferred distance depends on the specific $f(d)$ and $E$-bias functions, which have not been derived.

**Status:** 🔴 Open. No model to check.

## 4. BBN — Primordial Nucleosynthesis

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

R5 is a statement about emission-to-detection filtering in the present universe. It does not address early-universe conditions directly. However, R5's epistemic move (observed distributions are biased by selection) could in principle apply to the CMB and primordial abundance measurements as well — suggesting that our inferred early-universe conditions may themselves reflect selection biases. This is speculative and not developed.

**Status:** 🔴 Not addressed by R5 directly. Interesting but undeveloped epistemic extension.

## 5. CMB Blackbody Spectrum

**Severity:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-high.

A selection effect is blackbody-compatible if the selection is frequency-independent (or frequency-dependence averages out). For the geometric-aperture-bias version of R5, frequency independence is natural — the solid angle we subtend at a source is the same for all frequencies.

For the absorption-scattering-bias version, frequency-independence is harder — most absorption cross-sections are frequency-dependent. Some cancellation may occur at the level of cosmological path integrals.

**Status:** 🟢/🟡 Blackbody-compatible in the geometric version; 🟡 in the absorption-scattering version.

## 6. Tolman Surface Brightness

**Severity:** ⭐⭐ Medium.

The Tolman test measures surface brightness $\propto (1+z)^{-4}$ in expansion. In R5, the fraction of emitted flux reaching us as a function of distance is the selection fraction $f(d)$. Whether this reproduces $(1+z)^{-4}$ scaling depends on the specific $f(d)$.

A geometric-aperture bias alone ($f(d) \propto d^{-2}$ from solid-angle scaling) gives inverse-square falloff, which combines with other effects to yield the observed brightness scaling. Whether this matches $(1+z)^{-4}$ precisely is a quantitative question.

**Status:** 🟡 Plausibly consistent; requires specific $f(d)$ to check.

## 7. DESI DR2 — Weakening Dark Energy

**Severity:** ⭐ Low-medium.

R5 eliminates dark energy by reinterpreting the redshift-distance relation. DESI DR2 evolving $w(z)$ is consistent with R5's framework.

**Status:** 🟢 Supportive.

## The Key Strength

R5's ISW-cleanness is its biggest empirical advantage over R2. Where R2 must argue "the observed ISW signal is the graviton-density-gradient evolution, not $\Lambda$," R5 simply says "no ISW is produced by the mechanism; the observed signal needs an independent explanation." This is a weaker claim (doesn't explain the signal) but also a cleaner one (doesn't produce a conflicting signal).

## The Key Weakness

R5's time-dilation gap is the counterpart: it cannot touch SNe Ia time dilation at all. It is dependent on R6 in a way R2 is not — R2 has the Wiltshire precedent for how void/wall clock-rate differentials produce time dilation; R5 needs a completely separate story.
