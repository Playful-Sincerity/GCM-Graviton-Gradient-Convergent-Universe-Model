# Wolfram's Physics Project — Adjacent Framework GCM Needs to Position Against

Notes for when GCM theory development reaches the point of peer-review preparation or formal GR derivation. Not urgent now, but load-bearing later. If GCM publishes without engaging this territory, it reads as unaware rather than independent.

## The Closest Collision

Wolfram's Physics Project (2020–) and GCM are both **discrete-substrate unified theories of physics**. Both reject continuous spacetime as primitive. Both claim "one thing only, everything emerges." This is a direct adjacency, not a loose parallel.

Where they differ on the load-bearing point:
- **Wolfram's atoms of space are relational** — they have no location; connectivity *makes* location. Spacetime is the pattern of relations.
- **GCM's gravitons are material** — they have distances between them, which implies a pre-existing geometric substrate where they live.

This is the single question a Wolfram-literate reader would raise first: *what are GCM's gravitons embedded in?* If the answer is "space itself," you've hidden the thing you were supposed to derive.

## The Template GCM Could Adapt

**Jonathan Gorard** (Cambridge, Wolfram's primary physics collaborator) formally proved in arXiv:2004.14810 (published in *Journal of Complex Systems*, peer-reviewed):

- A discrete Ricci curvature tensor can be defined on hypergraphs
- Constraints on this tensor yield a discrete form of Einstein field equations
- In the continuum limit, these reduce to standard Einstein equations

This is not an analogy. It's a formal mathematical derivation that assumes only:
1. Hypergraph rewriting as substrate
2. Causal invariance of the rule (update-order independence)

**GCM needs exactly this kind of derivation of GR.** Currently the GCM theory documents assert that GR emerges at large scales from graviton density gradients, but the formal derivation is not written. The path forward:

1. **Reframe gravitons as atoms-of-space in Wolfram's sense** — relational elements whose connectivity constitutes space, rather than particles located in a pre-existing space.
2. **Treat "density gradient" as connectivity density** — regions of the hypergraph with more hyperedges per node.
3. **Use Gorard's derivation structure as a template** — the Ricci tensor work transfers if GCM's substrate can be cast as a hypergraph.
4. **Encode GCM's distinctive claim (convergence) as a preferred rulial slicing or symmetry-breaking** — the one place GCM genuinely adds to Wolfram's framework.

This is substantial work but it's not starting from zero. The derivation structure exists.

## The Convergence Question (Harder)

Wolfram's ruliad is horizontal — all possible rules, equally real, observers sample. GCM says the universe is vertical — converging under universal attraction toward final unity. These are not obviously compatible.

Two positions GCM could take:

**(A) Wolfram-friendly:** Convergence is what observers like us experience because of how we sample the ruliad. Different observers in different rulial slices might see arrows pointing other directions. GCM stays inside Wolfram's framework but loses its "universally true" flavor.

**(B) Ruliad-breaking:** The ruliad itself has directionality; not all samplings are equivalent. This is a stronger metaphysical claim that breaks Wolfram's frame. GCM gets to keep its cosmic directionality but has to argue against Wolfram's framework rather than within it.

**GCM currently hasn't decided.** The theory documents are ambiguous. This ambiguity is the single most important thing to resolve before publication. Either commits the project; ambiguity is the worst option.

## The QM Gap

Wolfram has a gesture-level mechanism for QM emergence via multiway systems + branchial space + observer sampling. Full Born rule derivation is not yet published — it's the weakest part of Wolfram's framework. GCM claims wave-particle duality is "real density gradients, not probability" but doesn't yet have a formal QM derivation either.

**Potentially interesting:** GCM's "real density gradient" interpretation of QM is more materially concrete than Wolfram's branchial-space interpretation. If GCM can produce a working QM derivation from graviton substrate, that's a move *Wolfram's camp cannot currently match*. It would be the strongest technical contribution GCM could make relative to the adjacent framework.

## What To Think About When You Get There

1. **Read Gorard's paper carefully** (arXiv:2004.14810 and the 100-page arXiv:2011.12174) before starting the formal GR derivation. The math structure is likely transferable.

2. **Decide convergence-vs-ruliad explicitly.** Write a section in the theory document that names the choice and defends it.

3. **Find GCM's distinctive empirical prediction.** Wolfram's framework predicts nothing currently testable (Planck-scale only). GCM's distinctiveness — the specific mechanism for cosmological redshift that produces time dilation for distant supernovae — is an empirical hook Wolfram lacks. Lead with this.

4. **Don't cite Wolfram in the abstract; cite him in §2.** The paper should stand on its own claims, then locate them in the landscape. Leading with "we extend Wolfram's framework" would be wrong (GCM isn't an extension); leading with "here's our theory, and here's how it relates to adjacent discrete-substrate approaches including Wolfram" is right.

5. **Speed-of-causality convention matters.** GCM's convention (photons move at $c - 0_x$, where $c$ is causality not light speed — see `theory/speed-of-causality-principle.md`) is distinctive and needs to be preserved across any framework translation. Wolfram's causal-graph formalism has a related but not identical notion of causal structure.
