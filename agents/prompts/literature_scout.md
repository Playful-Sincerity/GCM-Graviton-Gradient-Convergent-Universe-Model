# Prompt: Literature Scout (Wave 1)

**Agent**: general-purpose
**Run**: Background
**Date used**: 2026-03-21

---

You are a physics literature scout for a theoretical framework called GCM (Graviton Gradient Convergent Universe Model). Your task is to research two interconnected areas and produce a structured report. Do NOT write any code. Only search, read, and synthesize.

## The Framework's Core Claims (to research against)
1. One fundamental particle (the graviton) composes all matter
2. Gravity is the only fundamental force — EM, Strong, Weak emerge from gravitational density gradient interactions
3. Repulsion is not fundamental — it is "competing attraction" (differential gravitational pull)
4. The universe is gravitationally converging, not accelerating in expansion — current cosmological evidence is being misinterpreted
5. Particles are real spatial distributions (density gradients), not probability amplitudes — wavefunctions reflect real sub-structure

## AREA 1: PARTICLE PHYSICS

### 1a. Preon / Sub-lepton theories
- Rishon model (Harari, Shupe 1979)
- Other preon models (Pati-Salam, etc.)
- Current experimental constraints on electron sub-structure
- Why these theories fell out of favor
- What remains open / not ruled out

### 1b. Emergent gravity from a single substrate
- Erik Verlinde's entropic gravity (2010, 2016)
- Jacobson's thermodynamic derivation of GR (1995)
- Models where all four forces emerge from one field/particle
- String theory's single fundamental object

### 1c. Force unification and the symmetry problem
- Why Standard Model forces have different symmetries
- GUTs — how do they attempt unification?
- Parity violation problem
- Current status of quantum gravity approaches

### 1d. Repulsion as competing attraction
- Has anyone formally proposed EM repulsion as differential attraction to a background field?
- Screening in QED, running coupling constants
- Casimir effect and vacuum energy
