## 1. Direct Response to Bell and Parity

**Bell's Theorem**

The argument works only if GCM is committed to *local* hidden variables. Bell forecloses local realism, not realism as such. Bohmian mechanics is explicitly nonlocal and explicitly realistic, and is not ruled out by Bell. Graviton density gradients can be real and nonlocal. If gravitons attract across the entire universe, every gradient is intrinsically nonlocal — GCM is among the more natural homes for the kind of nonlocal realism Bohm already proved viable. The challenge identifies a constraint on GCM's formalization, not a refutation of its possibility.

**Parity Violation**

The weak force's left-handedness is a genuine unsolved derivation problem — not a falsification. GCM's Tenet 4 describes particles as density gradients with internal flow structure: charge is whether gravitons are flowing inward or outward. If particles have internal flow structure, configurations can be asymmetric about a reflection. A left-handed particle is a stable density configuration whose internal flow pattern has a preferred handedness — analogous to a vortex, which is chiral despite the underlying fluid being isotropic. Fluid mechanics is symmetric; vortices are not. This points to derivation work that must be done, but does not show the derivation is impossible.

## 2. Final Strongest Case

The debate has circled around a core asymmetry: my opponent's critiques have been critiques of *incompleteness*, not of *incoherence*. Bell requires a nonlocal formulation — achievable. Parity requires chiral emergent configurations — available in principle via flow asymmetries. The Coulomb-across-environments test is a genuine falsifiable prediction of the competing-attraction model. The void-growth objection assumed uniform convergence when GCM explicitly predicts asymmetric, hierarchical collapse.

Explanatory economy is a scientific criterion with a track record. When Maxwell unified electricity, magnetism, and optics, the unification was recognized as evidence before the derivations were complete. GCM's central bet is falsifiable in simulation right now. The N-body simulation has a specific near-term test: do stable particle-like clusters emerge, and does the inter-cluster force reproduce 1/r² at intermediate distances? That is a real prediction with a real test.

The convergence claim survives the void objection because GCM predicts hierarchical collapse with inter-cluster void expansion as a byproduct — which is in fact what large-scale structure surveys show. The question is whether this mechanism can quantitatively reproduce the supernova distance-luminosity relation. Falsifiable.

## 3. Concessions

The unification-history analogy was weaker than I presented. Maxwell and Einstein delivered the math. GCM's claim to unification is currently a promissory note, not a derivation.

Parity violation is harder than I would have liked. The vortex analogy is suggestive, not conclusive. Getting from isotropic graviton attraction to the exact SU(2) gauge structure with its specific fermion handedness coupling will require non-trivial theoretical machinery.

The thermodynamics parallel for the ethos argument was well-aimed. The more defensible claim is consonance, not entailment: a physics of attraction is *consonant* with an ethics of connection in a way that a physics of random decay is not.

GCM at v0.1 is a pre-formal theory with a specified ontology, a running simulation, and near-term falsifiable predictions. Bell and parity identify the hardest derivation problems on the critical path. Solving them is what the next phases are for. The framework is scientifically viable in the sense that matters: coherent ontology, identified derivation path, testable predictions. That is enough to justify the research.
